## ISSUE 1 — FULL DRAFT

**Subject / Headline:**
### The 5 AI Wins Michigan Businesses Are Leaving on the Table Right Now

---

If you're a Michigan manufacturer, practice manager, fleet operator, or contractor reading this — there's a good chance AI is already on your radar.

You've probably had the conversation with leadership. Maybe you've tried ChatGPT. Maybe you've seen the vendor demos. And maybe you've come away thinking: *"This doesn't really apply to us."*

It does. Here's what I'm seeing from the ground.

---

**Win #1 — Quality reporting that writes itself**

A Tier 2 automotive supplier spent 22 hours a week pulling defect data from three systems and building a PDF report for their OEM customer.

We connected the systems, built a dashboard, and automated the weekly output. The report now generates itself on Friday morning. The quality team uses the 22 hours to actually fix the problems instead of document them.

Time to build: 6 weeks. Going PRO grant covered 50% of the cost.

---

**Win #2 — Prior auth without the phone hold music**

A Michigan medical practice had two staff members spending 28 hours a week on prior authorizations — most of it waiting on hold and re-entering the same information across systems.

An AI workflow now handles the initial submission, follows up on pending auths, and escalates only the genuinely complex cases to staff. Prior auth time dropped from 28 hours to 6 hours per week. Denial rate went from 11% to 4%.

The system is HIPAA-compliant. No cloud access to patient data. Built on the practice's own server.

---

**Win #3 — Deadhead miles that don't come back empty**

A 45-truck Michigan carrier was running 22% deadhead — about $180K in empty miles per year.

AI dispatch optimization, connected to their TMS, matched loads better and cut deadhead to 16%. That's $108K recovered. The system also improved on-time delivery by 14%, which opened two new lane contracts.

Implementation paid for itself in 11 weeks.

---

**Win #4 — Bids that take 3 hours instead of 14**

A Southeast Michigan GC was spending 14 hours per bid — pulling historical costs, formatting estimates, writing the scope. Their estimator was maxed out at 5 bids per month.

AI-assisted estimating cut that to 3 hours. Now they bid 9 projects per month. Win rate stayed the same. Revenue opportunity nearly doubled.

They're also capturing change orders automatically — every field decision gets logged, photographed, and drafted into a CO before it becomes a dispute.

---

**Win #5 — The one nobody talks about: the grant math**

Michigan has two active funding programs that cover AI implementation costs:

- **Going PRO Talent Fund** — Up to $50K reimbursement for AI training, per business, per year. Most Michigan businesses have never applied.
- **Industry 4.0 Tech Grant** — 50% reimbursement on implementation costs for small manufacturers.

A $15,000 AI project can net out to $3,500–$11,000 after grants.

Most vendors don't tell you this because they don't know Michigan's funding landscape. We work it into every proposal.
