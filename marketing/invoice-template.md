# Invoice Template
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** Every dollar earned needs a professional invoice that matches the brand, references the signed agreement, and gives the client everything they need to pay quickly. A confusing or unprofessional invoice slows payment. A clean invoice with clear payment instructions gets paid fast.
**Send via:** Pandadoc Payments (ACH + credit card), Stripe Invoice, or HoneyBook — all generate payment links. Never send a PDF with bank details in email as the only option.
**Payment terms:** Net 15 from invoice date (unless otherwise specified in the SOW).

# INVOICE TIMING — THE RULE

**Deposit invoice:** Send on the same day the client signs the MSA and SOW. Not the next day — the same day. Pandadoc can trigger this automatically when a document is signed.

**Final invoice:** Send on the day of final delivery, when the client confirms the system is live and accepted. Not after a "wrap up period" — on delivery day.

**Late invoices signal a poorly run operation.** Invoice same-day, every time.

# PAYMENT FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

**Day 1:** Invoice sent via Pandadoc/Stripe with payment link

**Day 8 (7 days before due):** Auto-reminder from Pandadoc or Stripe — "Invoice AAIS-2026-001 is due in 7 days. Pay online: [link]"

**Day 16 (1 day past due):** Email from David:

> Subject: Invoice AAIS-2026-001 — due date passed
>
> [Name] —
>
> Invoice AAIS-2026-001 for $[X] was due [yesterday]. Sending a quick follow-up in case it got buried.
>
> Pay online here: [link] — or let me know if you prefer a different payment method.
>
> David

**Day 23 (7 days past due):** Call. Don't email. A 90-second phone call:
> "[Name], calling about invoice AAIS-2026-001 for $[X] — it's now 7 days past due. Can you tell me where it is in your payment process? I want to make sure it's not stuck somewhere."

**Day 30 (14 days past due):** Formal written notice that interest is accruing per the MSA. Pause any ongoing retainer work until resolved.

**Most invoices are paid before Day 16.** The sequence above rarely gets past Day 8.

# HANDLING "CAN YOU SEND A DIFFERENT FORMAT?"

Some clients (especially large manufacturers or healthcare systems) have AP departments that require specific formats — PO numbers, specific vendor IDs, W-9 on file.

**Before sending the first invoice, ask:**
> "Before I send the invoice — does your AP department need anything specific? A PO number, a W-9, or a particular invoice format?"

This prevents the "our AP can't process this" delay that costs 2–3 weeks.

**Always have ready:**
- [ ] Signed W-9 (current year) — PDF in Google Drive, send on request
- [ ] Certificate of Organization from Michigan LARA (proof of business entity)
- [ ] Vendor setup form — fill out whatever their AP department sends

The W-9 in particular gets requested by almost every corporate client before the first payment. Have it signed and current before the first invoice goes out.
