# Year 2 Expansion Playbook
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** Most AI consultants do a project, get paid, and never go back. That's leaving $40,000–$80,000 per client on the table. This playbook is the system for turning every closed client into a 2–5 year revenue relationship.
**When to start:** Begin planting seeds at the 30-day check-in. Start the expansion conversation at the 90-day check-in. Close Year 2 scope at 6–9 months.
**Revenue math:** 1 client at $15,000 Year 1 → $8,000–$30,000 Year 2 → $6,000–$12,000 Year 3 retainer. Total: $29,000–$57,000 over 3 years from a single $15,000 project.

---

## WHY CLIENTS EXPAND (AND WHY THEY DON'T)

**Clients expand when:**
- The first system worked — they can point to a number that changed
- They trust you more than they trusted anyone before the project
- A new problem becomes visible *because* the first system surfaced data they didn't have
- A new grant cycle opens and they want to move fast
- A competitor does something with AI and creates urgency

**Clients don't expand when:**
- You delivered the system but didn't document what it changed (no numbers = no momentum)
- You went quiet after handoff — no 30-day check-in, no 90-day call
- They don't know what else you could build for them
- They had a rough experience (even minor) that was never acknowledged and resolved

**The rule:** If a client doesn't expand, 80% of the time it's a relationship problem, not a budget problem. Stay present. Call. Show up with ideas.

## THE EXPANSION ROADMAP BY VERTICAL

### AUTOMOTIVE TIER 2/3 SUPPLIERS

**Year 1 project (typical):** Quality dashboard / OEE monitor — $12,000–$18,000

**Year 2 natural expansions:**

**1. Supplier Scorecard Automation**
- What it is: Automatically pull quality data from the Year 1 dashboard + purchasing data from ERP → generate monthly supplier performance report (on-time delivery, defect rate, price variance) that used to take 3 days by hand
- Trigger: Ask at 60-day check-in: "How long does it take your team to prepare the monthly supplier review?"
- Scope: $8,000–$14,000 / 4–6 weeks
- Grant angle: Going PRO (training on new supplier evaluation process)

**2. Predictive Maintenance Layer**
- What it is: Add anomaly detection to the existing OEE monitor — catch equipment degradation 2–3 days before failure using sensor data patterns
- Trigger: If they've had any unplanned downtime since deployment, this sells itself
- Scope: $12,000–$20,000 / 5–8 weeks
- Grant angle: Industry 4.0 Tech Grant (equipment + AI integration)

**3. Audit-Ready Document Generation**
- What it is: AI that pulls from the quality dashboard and auto-generates IATF/Ford Q1/GM BIQS audit preparation packets
- Trigger: "When's your next OEM audit? How long does prep typically take?"
- Scope: $8,000–$15,000 / 3–5 weeks

**4. Retainer: System Maintenance + Monthly Data Review**
- What it is: Monthly 90-minute review call + dashboard maintenance + model retraining as production mix changes
- Price: $1,500–$3,000/month
- Pitch: "Your line mix will change. The AI needs to adapt. Here's how we keep it calibrated."

## THE EXPANSION CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK

The worst way to sell Year 2 is with a proposal deck. The best way is a question.

### At the 30-Day Check-In

Don't pitch. Ask:

> "Now that you've been using the system for a month — what's the next thing on your list that's still manual and painful?"

Let them answer. Write it down. Say: "That's interesting. I have a few ideas about how we could approach that — let me think about it and I'll send you something."

Then send a 3-paragraph email with a rough scope and price range. Not a proposal — a paragraph.

### At the 90-Day Check-In

By now you have results data. Use it.

> "[Name], you mentioned when we started that [the original pain point]. Based on what we've tracked over 90 days: [specific result]. That's the baseline. Now I want to show you where the next 10 points of improvement are."

Come with 2–3 specific ideas from the expansion list above. Frame each one as:
- What it is (one sentence)
- What it would change (the specific number)
- Rough scope and price
- Whether a grant applies

### The Language That Works

**Don't say:** "I'd love to propose a new project."
**Do say:** "Based on what we've learned from your data, here's where I think the next $[X] of value is hiding."

**Don't say:** "We could build a dashboard for [X]."
**Do say:** "Your team is still spending [Y hours] on [Z process]. We could eliminate most of that."

**Don't say:** "Do you have budget for Year 2?"
**Do say:** "The Q[X] Going PRO cycle closes [date]. If we scope this before then, you can stack the grant on this project the same way we did the first one."

## THE RETAINER PITCH

Retainers are the most predictable revenue in a consulting business. Every client who has a live AI system is a retainer candidate.

**What a retainer covers (customize by client):**
- Monthly model performance review (30 minutes — is the AI still accurate?)
- Model retraining as data changes (quarterly)
- Bug fixes and minor modifications (<4 hours/month)
- On-call support for staff questions
- Vendor API management (if system uses Claude/OpenAI — rate limits, pricing changes, etc.)
- Annual BAA renewal support (healthcare clients)

**Pricing:**
- Basic: $1,000–$1,500/month — maintenance only, no new development
- Standard: $1,500–$2,500/month — maintenance + 2 hours development/month
- Growth: $2,500–$4,000/month — maintenance + 5 hours development + quarterly expansion call

**The retainer pitch script:**
> "The system we built runs on APIs that update and change. The data it learns from changes as your business changes. Right now I'm the person who knows exactly how this is configured — if something breaks or needs updating without me, it's going to be a hard fix. The retainer is essentially you keeping me on call as the system's architect. Most clients find it cheaper than the alternative."

**When to pitch the retainer:**
- Offer it as part of the original project (at proposal stage): "Optional: ongoing maintenance retainer — $X/month."
- If they don't take it during the project, offer it at the 30-day check-in: "Now that it's live, do you want me to manage the ongoing maintenance, or do you want to handle that internally?"

## TRACKING EXPANSION OPPORTUNITIES IN HUBSPOT

Add a custom deal property: **"Expansion Opportunity"** (dropdown)
- Options: None identified, Year 2 scoped, Year 2 proposed, Year 2 won, Retainer active

**Workflow:** 30 days after "Won" deal → trigger "Expansion check-in" task → log outcome → update property

**Expansion pipeline (separate from new business pipeline):**
- Stage 1: Active Client (system live, 30-day check-in done)
- Stage 2: Expansion Identified (pain point surfaced at 30-day or 90-day call)
- Stage 3: Expansion Scoped (informal paragraph sent)
- Stage 4: Expansion Proposed (formal scope/price sent)
- Stage 5: Expansion Won
- Stage 6: Retainer Active
