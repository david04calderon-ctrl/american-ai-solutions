## VERTICAL 1: AUTOMOTIVE TIER 2/3 SUPPLIERS

### Who exactly to target

**Company type:**
- Stamping, injection molding, machining, assembly, welding shops
- Metal fabrication, forging, casting operations
- Electrical components, wire harness, interior trim
- Quality testing / inspection subcontractors

**Size sweet spot:** 25–300 employees, $3M–$75M revenue
**Location priority:** Southeast Michigan (Wayne, Oakland, Macomb, Livingston, Monroe counties)

**Title targets (in order of authority):**
1. Quality Manager / Director of Quality (OEM-facing — feels the pain most acutely)
2. VP of Operations / COO (budget authority)
3. Plant Manager (day-to-day operational visibility)
4. CEO / President (smaller shops — often wears all three hats)

---

### How to find them

**Source 1 — Michigan Manufacturers Directory**
- michigan.org/manufacturers-directory
- Filter by NAICS code: 3360-3369 (Motor vehicle parts)
- Also: 3320-3329 (Fabricated metal), 3330-3339 (Machinery)

**Source 2 — Automotive News Michigan Supplier Database**
- autonews.com (paid) — worth the month subscription for list building

**Source 3 — LinkedIn Sales Navigator**
- Search: "Quality Manager" + Michigan + 10–500 employees + "Automotive" or "Manufacturing"
- Also search: "Operations Manager" + Michigan + "Tier 2" OR "Tier 3"

**Source 4 — Michigan's Economic Development Corporation (MEDC)**
- Pure Michigan Business Connect: puremichiganbc.com
- Lists certified Michigan suppliers with contact info

**Source 5 — OEM Supplier directories**
- GM Supplier Partners: gmsupplierpartners.com
- Ford Supplier Portal (public-facing supplier search)
- Stellantis supplier directory

**Source 6 — Clay (automated enrichment)**
- Import company list → waterfall enrichment (LinkedIn + Clearbit + Hunter.io)
- Add Clay column: "Recent OEM audit news" — flag for urgent outreach

---

### Qualification triggers (prioritize these leads)

- Company posts about OEM audit prep, IATF 16949, APQP, or PPAP documentation
- Recent job posting for Quality Inspector or Quality Engineer (data problem signal)
- Plant manager or quality manager changed in the last 6 months (new decision maker = fresh conversations)
- OEM supplier certification recently awarded or renewed (data requirements follow)
- LinkedIn post about production challenges or quality concerns

---

### Disqualifiers

- Tier 1 suppliers (too large, have internal IT)
- OEM subsidiaries (internal bureaucracy)
- Primarily defense contractors (different compliance universe)

## VERTICAL 2: MICHIGAN HEALTHCARE PRACTICES

### Who exactly to target

**Practice type:**
- Multi-provider primary care (3–15 physicians)
- Orthopedics, cardiology, oncology specialty practices
- Behavioral health / mental health practices
- Physical therapy and rehabilitation groups
- Urgent care groups (multi-location)
- Independent radiology practices

**Size sweet spot:** 3–20 providers, 1–5 locations
**Location priority:** Detroit metro (Wayne, Oakland, Macomb) + Grand Rapids + Lansing + Flint

**Title targets:**
1. Practice Manager / Office Manager (day-to-day ops, often initiates technology discussions)
2. Medical Director (clinical authority — needed for HIPAA/AI sign-off)
3. COO (multi-location groups)
4. Physician-owner (small independent practices)

---

### How to find them

**Source 1 — Michigan Medicine organization finder**
- Find Independent practices through Michigan Primary Care Alliance (MPCA)
- Michigan Osteopathic Association member directory

**Source 2 — MGMA (Medical Group Management Association)**
- Michigan chapter: mgma.com/michigan
- Member directory includes practice size and contact info

**Source 3 — LinkedIn**
- Search: "Practice Manager" + Michigan + Healthcare + [specialty]
- Search: "Medical Director" + Michigan + "independent practice" (filter out hospital employees)

**Source 4 — Michigan Health & Hospital Association (MHA)**
- mha.org — member directory, focus on independent practices (not hospital systems)

**Source 5 — NPI Registry (NPPES)**
- nppes.cms.hhs.gov — search by specialty, state, city
- Returns practice name, address, and sometimes phone
- Cross-reference with LinkedIn for contact person

**Source 6 — Health plan provider directories**
- BCBS Michigan provider search — filter for independent practices in specific geo

---

### Qualification triggers

- Job posting for "Prior Authorization Coordinator" (the pain is active)
- Practice is independent (not hospital-owned — budget autonomy)
- 3+ providers (enough volume for AI ROI to work)
- Recent HIPAA audit or documentation requirement from payer
- LinkedIn post about staffing challenges, burnout, or administrative burden
- Specialty with high prior auth burden: cardiology, orthopedics, behavioral health, oncology

---

### Disqualifiers

- Hospital-employed practices (no budget autonomy, long procurement cycles)
- Solo practitioners (insufficient volume for ROI)
- FQHCs (federally qualified health centers — different grant structure)
- Large national groups (Ascension, Spectrum, Henry Ford — internal IT)

## VERTICAL 3: MICHIGAN LOGISTICS / TRUCKING

### Who exactly to target

**Company type:**
- OTR (over-the-road) carriers with Michigan base
- LTL regional carriers
- Specialized haulers (flatbed, temperature-controlled, hazmat)
- Freight brokers with dispatch operations
- Last-mile delivery operations (20+ routes)

**Size sweet spot:** 20–200 trucks, 15–150 drivers
**Location priority:** I-75, I-94, I-96 corridors — Detroit, Flint, Lansing, Grand Rapids, Kalamazoo

**Title targets:**
1. Director of Operations / VP of Operations (budget + urgency)
2. Dispatch Manager (operational pain — most motivated)
3. Fleet Manager (equipment + driver data visibility)
4. Owner / President (smaller carriers — often the dispatcher too)

---

### How to find them

**Source 1 — FMCSA Motor Carrier database (free, powerful)**
- li.fmcsa.dot.gov/liview/pkg_carrquery.prc_carrlist
- Search by state: Michigan
- Filter by: Active carriers, 20+ power units (trucks)
- Export includes company name, address, phone, MC number
- Cross-reference with SAFER system for safety record

**Source 2 — Michigan Trucking Association (MTA)**
- mitrucking.org — member directory
- Members are more engaged in the industry and open to conversations

**Source 3 — LinkedIn**
- Search: "Director of Operations" + Michigan + Trucking OR "Transportation"
- Search: "Dispatch Manager" + Michigan + Logistics
- Filter by company size: 11–200 employees

**Source 4 — Load boards (indirect targeting)**
- DAT and Truckstop.com have carrier directories
- Focus on carriers with Michigan authority + consistent Michigan lanes

**Source 5 — GreatLakesTrucking.com and Michigan logistics trade publications**
- Forum members and advertisers are active in the market

---

### Qualification triggers

- Fleet size 20–120 trucks (sweet spot for AI ROI)
- Recent job posting for dispatcher (hiring = overwhelmed = AI need)
- Company expanded fleet in last 12 months (growth + new dispatch complexity)
- Operating in Michigan freight corridors (automotive parts, retail, food distribution)
- Owner-operator who's built a fleet (entrepreneurial, fast decision-maker)

---

### Disqualifiers

- Owner-operators with 1–5 trucks (too small for dispatch AI ROI)
- National mega-carriers with 10,000+ trucks (have internal tech teams)
- Carriers with custom TMS that can't be integrated (rare but happens)

## VERTICAL 4: MICHIGAN CONSTRUCTION / TRADES

### Who exactly to target

**Company type:**
- General contractors (commercial and light industrial focus)
- Specialty trades: mechanical, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing
- Heavy civil: site prep, excavation, concrete
- Subcontractors with their own estimating function

**Size sweet spot:** 10–150 employees, $2M–$50M revenue
**Location priority:** Detroit metro + Grand Rapids + Traverse City + Upper Peninsula (less competition)

**Title targets:**
1. President / Owner (often also the estimator — direct budget authority)
2. VP of Operations or Director of Preconstruction (estimating oversight)
3. Chief Estimator (feels the pain directly)
4. Project Executive (larger GCs — oversees multiple projects)

---

### How to find them

**Source 1 — Associated General Contractors of Michigan (AGC)**
- agcmichigan.org — member directory by specialty
- Members tend to be growth-oriented and open to tools

**Source 2 — Michigan Builders Exchange**
- Search by specialty and geography
- Also: plan rooms — GCs who bid actively will be in there

**Source 3 — Dodge Data / ConstructConnect**
- Bidding history shows which GCs are active and growing
- Focus on GCs bidding commercial, industrial, and healthcare construction

**Source 4 — LinkedIn**
- Search: "President" + Michigan + "General Contractor" OR "Construction"
- Search: "Estimator" + Michigan + Construction (reach them where they feel the pain)
- Filter company size: 10–200

**Source 5 — State of Michigan construction permits**
- County recorder offices + building permit databases
- Active permit pullers = active contractors = decision-makers with revenue

**Source 6 — Michigan Subcontractors Association**
- michigansubcontractors.org — specialty trade members

---

### Qualification triggers

- GC bids more than 20 projects per year (estimating bottleneck is real)
- Recently hired or lost an estimator (reorganizing = open to tools)
- Specializes in medical, commercial, or industrial work (types with high drawing complexity)
- Company is growing — expanded headcount, new equipment, new locations
- Complaint about Procore or Buildertrend complexity (open to workflow changes)

---

### Disqualifiers

- Residential-only GCs (different workflow, lower project values, lower AI ROI)
- One-man operations
- Purely public-sector GCs (procurement restrictions may apply)

## LEAD SCORING QUICK REFERENCE

Score each lead 1–10 before outreach. High scores get phone calls + emails. Low scores get email-only.

| Signal | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Decision maker (not gatekeeper) | +3 |
| Right size (10–500 employees) | +2 |
| Active pain signal (job posting, LinkedIn, news) | +3 |
| Michigan-based | +1 |
| Prior contact / warm | +3 |
| No visible tech stack / paper-based signals | +2 |
| Wrong size (too large or too small) | −2 |
| Hospital system / corporate subsidiary | −3 |
| Outside Michigan | −1 |

**Score 7+:** High priority — call + email sequence
**Score 5–6:** Medium — email sequence only
**Score <5:** Low — add to newsletter/content only

## WHERE TO FIND GRANT-ELIGIBLE CLIENTS FIRST

The fastest path to closed deals: find companies that are already aware of Michigan grants and are looking for someone to help them use the money.

**Sources:**
1. **Michigan Works! offices** — They help employers apply for Going PRO. Ask if they can refer employers who've been told they qualify for AI training but don't know where to start.
2. **Michigan SBDC (Small Business Development Centers)** — Counselors at SBDCs are actively helping Michigan businesses find grant money. A referral partner relationship here is worth 10 cold emails.
3. **MEDC (Michigan Economic Development Corporation)** — Handles Industry 4.0 grants. They're looking for implementation partners to refer.
4. **Michigan Manufacturing Technology Center (MMTC)** — Government-funded consulting for Michigan manufacturers. They refer to implementation specialists.

**Pitch for referral partners:**
> "I build the AI systems that make Michigan businesses eligible for the Going PRO and Industry 4.0 grants. I handle all the grant documentation. If you're working with manufacturers or healthcare practices who want to move on AI but need a vetted implementation partner, I'd love to be a resource for your clients."
