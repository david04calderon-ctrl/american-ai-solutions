# Press Announcement & Credibility Template
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** When you launch, close a significant client, reach a milestone, or do something newsworthy, you need a ready-to-deploy announcement. This guide covers press releases for Detroit-area business media, LinkedIn announcement posts, and the "social proof stack" strategy — how to build credibility signals even before you have a full client roster.
**Timing:** Issue press releases when you have real news. Announcing "we launched" with no clients is weak. Announcing "we closed our first healthcare client" or "we're the first Michigan-based AI firm to achieve [X]" is news.

## THE CREDIBILITY STACK — BUILDING BEFORE YOU HAVE CLIENTS

Most founders wait for clients to build credibility. The smart move is to build credibility signals *before* — so when the first prospect Googles you, they find a legitimate operation.

**In order of impact:**

**1. Website live (Week 1)** — americanaisolutionsllc.com published. Every prospect will check this.

**2. Google My Business published (Week 1)** — Shows up when someone Googles "American AI Solutions LLC Detroit." Establishes local presence.

**3. LinkedIn company page live (Week 1)** — Two-way link with personal profile.

**4. Blog published (Week 2)** — 3+ articles indexed by Google. Shows expertise.

**5. LinkedIn content cadence started (Week 1)** — 3 posts/week. Even with 50 followers, prospects who check your profile see active thought leadership.

**6. Podcast or media appearance (Month 2–3)** — Even a small Michigan podcast episode creates a backlink and a shareable credibility signal. See podcast-speaking-strategy.md.

**7. First case study published (Month 2–3)** — The single biggest credibility jump. One anonymized case study with real numbers beats 10 testimonials.

**8. First Google review (Month 2–3)** — Ask warm contacts to leave a Google review. Even 3 reviews is better than zero.

**9. Press release picked up (Month 2–3)** — Even a mention in Crain's Detroit creates a credibility signal you can link to in proposals.

**10. Award / recognition (Month 6+)** — Apply for "Michigan 40 Under 40," Crain's "Notable in Technology," Detroit Regional Chamber programs. These are not vanity — they're Google results that stay forever.

## MILESTONE PRESS RELEASE TRIGGERS

Don't send a press release for every small win. Send for real milestones:

| Milestone | Press worthiness |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Company launch | Low — only relevant if story is strong |
| First client delivered | Medium — lead with results, not the announcement |
| First $100K revenue | High — "Detroit AI firm reaches [X] clients, $100K revenue" |
| Michigan grant milestone | High — "AAIS helps Michigan businesses recover $[X] in grant funding" |
| Partnership (MMTC, Michigan Works!) | Medium — local angle, co-announcement with partner |
| Vertical expansion | Medium — if entering a new market (e.g., healthcare to agriculture) |
| Team hire | Low unless the hire is newsworthy (C-suite, known figure) |
| Award / recognition | Always — awards are designed to generate press |

**The rule:** Every press release needs a news angle that would make someone else say "that's interesting." "We launched" is not interesting. "Detroit AI firm saves Michigan manufacturers $X in the first year" is interesting.
