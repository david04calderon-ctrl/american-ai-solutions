## PROGRAM OVERVIEW — GOING PRO TALENT FUND

**Program manager:** Michigan Department of Labor and Economic Opportunity (LEO)
**Website:** michigan.gov/leo/bureaus-agencies/wd/going-pro
**Administered by:** Michigan Works! agencies (28 across the state — your client applies through their local Michigan Works!)
**Cycle:** Applications accepted quarterly (January, April, July, October start dates)
**Funds available:** Approximately $6 million annually, first-come-first-served per region
**Reimbursement rate:** Up to 50% of eligible training costs (not to exceed $50,000/employer/year)

**Why AI projects qualify:**
Going PRO was designed for employer-sponsored training that upgrades worker skills. AI implementation inherently includes:
- Training employees to use the new AI system (documented in AAIS SOWs)
- Developing new skills in data interpretation and AI-assisted workflows
- Creating organizational capability that didn't exist before

The key is how the project is documented — which is where AAIS's grant expertise adds direct value.

## ELIGIBILITY CHECKLIST

Before starting the application, confirm the client meets all criteria:

**Employer eligibility:**
- [ ] Michigan-based employer (any location in the state)
- [ ] For-profit or non-profit (government entities not eligible)
- [ ] Registered with Michigan Unemployment Insurance Agency (most businesses already are)
- [ ] Current on all state tax obligations
- [ ] Not in bankruptcy proceedings

**Training eligibility:**
- [ ] Training benefits current Michigan employees (not new hires hired specifically for training)
- [ ] Training is customized to employer's operations (not generic off-the-shelf courses)
- [ ] Training results in a skill upgrade documented with a credential, certificate, or documented competency
- [ ] Training has a minimum of 8 hours per employee
- [ ] Employer pays the training provider directly (AAIS) and seeks reimbursement

**Industry notes:**
- Manufacturing: Highest approval rate — explicitly named in Going PRO materials
- Healthcare: Qualifies when training upgrades clinical or administrative staff skills
- Logistics/Trucking: Qualifies when training improves operational efficiency for Michigan-based employees
- Construction: Qualifies when training improves technical skills of Michigan employees

## THE APPLICATION PROCESS — STEP BY STEP

### Step 1: Find the Right Michigan Works! Agency

Each client applies through the Michigan Works! agency serving their county. Find the agency at: michiganworks.org/find-your-local-michigan-works

**Detroit/Wayne County:** Detroit Employment Solutions Corporation (DESC)
**Oakland County:** Oakland County Michigan Works!
**Macomb County:** Macomb Michigan Works!
**Kent County (Grand Rapids):** West Michigan Works!
**Washtenaw County:** Washtenaw County Michigan Works!
**Genesee County (Flint):** Genesee County Michigan Works!

**David's role:** Identify the correct agency for the client's location and provide them the contact name and phone number.

### Step 2: Pre-Application Contact

Before submitting, clients should call their Michigan Works! agency and say:

> "I'm an employer interested in applying for Going PRO funding for AI system training. We're working with a Michigan-based AI firm to implement a custom system for our operation. I'd like to understand the current application window and what documentation you'll need."

This does two things:
1. Establishes the application before the window fills
2. Gets a named contact at the agency who can flag the application for attention

**David's role:** Provide the client with the script above and the agency contact. Offer to join the first call if the client wants support.

### Step 3: Prepare the Training Plan Document

This is the most important document in the application — and the one AAIS prepares for every client.

**The Training Plan must include:**

**Section A: Employer Information**
- Company name, EIN, NAICS code, Michigan Works! agency, number of Michigan employees

**Section B: Training Overview**
- Training provider: American AI Solutions LLC
- Training type: Customized employer-specific AI systems training
- Delivery method: On-site and remote instruction
- Training dates: [Project start date] through [Project end date + 30-day support period]
- Total training hours per employee: [X] hours
- Number of employees trained: [Y]

**Section C: Skill Upgrades**
This section explains what skills employees are gaining that they didn't have before. For AAIS projects, use language like:

*Automotive clients:*
> "Employees will develop competency in operating AI-assisted quality monitoring systems, interpreting real-time OEE dashboards, executing AI-driven defect detection workflows, and generating OEM-compliant data reports using integrated AI tools. These skills represent a measurable upgrade from manual quality inspection and spreadsheet-based reporting."

*Healthcare clients:*
> "Clinical and administrative staff will develop competency in operating HIPAA-compliant AI-assisted prior authorization workflows, using AI-generated clinical documentation tools, interpreting AI-flagged alerts for care coordination, and managing AI-assisted patient communication workflows. These skills represent a measurable upgrade from fully manual administrative processes."

*Logistics clients:*
> "Dispatch and operations staff will develop competency in operating AI-assisted load matching systems, interpreting AI-generated route optimization recommendations, using AI dashboards for deadhead tracking and lane profitability analysis, and managing exception-based dispatch workflows. These skills represent a measurable upgrade from manual dispatch decision-making."

*Construction clients:*
> "Project estimators and project managers will develop competency in operating AI-assisted takeoff and estimating workflows, using AI-generated change order capture tools, interpreting AI-driven bid analysis outputs, and managing AI-assisted subcontractor bid comparison. These skills represent a measurable upgrade from fully manual estimating and change order documentation."

**Section D: Training Outcomes**
How will you verify employees learned the skills?
- Written competency assessment (AAIS provides a 10-question post-training assessment)
- Demonstrated system operation (employee runs a complete workflow without assistance)
- 30-day performance review (supervisor documents employee is operating the system independently)

**Section E: Cost Breakdown**

| Line item | Cost | Going PRO eligible? |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| AI system development | $[X] | Partially (training component) |
| Employee training (on-site instruction) | $[Y] | Yes — 100% |
| Training materials + documentation | $[Z] | Yes — 100% |
| 30-day post-training support | $[W] | Yes — 100% |
| **Total eligible training costs** | **$[Total]** | |
| **Reimbursement requested (50%)** | **$[Total/2]** | |

**David's role:** Prepare this entire document in a clean Word or Google Doc template for each client. Fill in project-specific details from the SOW. Review together on a 20-minute call before client submits.

### Step 4: Prepare Supporting Documents

Along with the Training Plan, the application requires:

**From the employer (client collects):**
- [ ] Federal EIN verification (EIN letter from IRS or payroll stub header)
- [ ] List of employees to be trained (names + job titles — does not need to include SSNs)
- [ ] Proof of Michigan UIA registration (quarterly wage report header)
- [ ] Employer signature on application form

**From the training provider (AAIS provides):**
- [ ] AAIS W-9 (signed, current year)
- [ ] AAIS business registration in Michigan (copy of LLC operating agreement or Certificate of Organization from Michigan LARA)
- [ ] Training provider profile: brief description of AAIS, David's background, relevant expertise
- [ ] Sample training curriculum / agenda (AAIS provides a standardized version per project)

**David maintains a "Grant Document Packet" folder** in Google Drive with all reusable AAIS documents. Only the training plan and employee list change project to project.

---

### Step 5: Submit the Application

Applications are submitted to the Michigan Works! agency, not directly to LEO. The agency reviews, approves, and processes reimbursement.

**Submission method:** Most Michigan Works! agencies accept email submissions. Some have online portals. Confirm with the agency contact identified in Step 2.

**Timeline after submission:**
- Agency review: 2–4 weeks
- Approval notification: Agency contacts the employer
- Project must start after approval (not before — this is a common disqualifier)
- Project completion: By end of the grant period (typically the end of the quarter or fiscal year)
- Reimbursement claim submitted: After project completion with receipts/invoices
- Reimbursement payment: 4–8 weeks after claim submission

**Critical:** The project cannot start until the application is approved. In practice, AAIS structures projects so:
- Application submitted: 2–4 weeks before planned project start
- Project start: After written approval received
- This sequence is built into every project timeline

### Step 6: During the Project — Documentation Requirements

During the project, maintain records that will support the reimbursement claim:

**AAIS maintains and provides to client:**
- [ ] Training session attendance log (signed by each employee after each training session)
- [ ] Training session agendas (what was covered in each session)
- [ ] Pre- and post-training competency assessments
- [ ] Final invoice from AAIS to client (clearly separating training vs. development line items)
- [ ] Proof of payment from client to AAIS

**Client maintains:**
- [ ] Proof of payment to AAIS (bank statement or cancelled check)
- [ ] Employee timesheets showing training hours
- [ ] Supervisor sign-off on employee competency post-training

---

### Step 7: Submit the Reimbursement Claim

After project completion, submit to the Michigan Works! agency:

- Completed reimbursement claim form (agency provides)
- AAIS invoice(s) for the project
- Proof of payment to AAIS
- Training attendance logs (signed)
- Copies of competency assessment results
- Any additional documentation the agency requests

**Reimbursement is paid** directly to the employer, typically by check or ACH, 4–8 weeks after claim submission.

## INDUSTRY 4.0 TECH GRANT — ADDITIONAL REIMBURSEMENT

For manufacturing clients, a second grant opportunity stacks on top of Going PRO.

**Program:** Michigan Industry 4.0 Technology Grant
**Administrator:** Michigan Economic Development Corporation (MEDC) + Michigan Manufacturing Technology Center (MMTC)
**Amount:** Up to $25,000 (50% reimbursement of eligible implementation costs); requires SBA small business designation and prior MMTC assessment
**Eligible costs:** Technology implementation, integration, and associated training

**Key difference from Going PRO:**
- Going PRO reimburses training costs (50%)
- Industry 4.0 reimburses the full technology implementation (50% of total project cost)
- Both can be claimed on the same project — for different cost categories

**For a $20,000 AAIS manufacturing project:**
- Industry 4.0: 50% of $12,000 development costs = $6,000 reimbursement
- Going PRO: 50% of $8,000 training costs = $4,000 reimbursement
- Total grant recovery: $10,000
- Net client cost: $10,000

**Application process:** Contact MMTC directly — mmtc.org — they walk manufacturers through the application and often co-brand the announcement, which gives the client additional credibility.
