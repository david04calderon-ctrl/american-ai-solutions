## SLIDE 2 — THE BLEEDING POINTS

**Headline:** Where the margin is actually going.

**The three biggest AI-solvable cost drivers in Michigan trucking:**

**1. Empty Miles (Deadhead)** — Industry average is 17–22% of total miles. At $2.50/mile average cost, a 50-truck fleet running 500,000 miles/year is burning $212,000–$275,000 in empty miles alone. AI load matching cuts this by 40–60%.

**2. Manual Dispatch & Load Planning** — Dispatchers spending 3–4 hours/day on tasks that AI handles in minutes: load matching, route optimization, driver assignment, check calls. At 2 dispatchers × $55K/year, you're paying $110K for work that scales badly.

**3. Driver Turnover** — Michigan trucking averages 78% annual driver turnover. Each replacement costs $5,000–$12,000 in recruiting, onboarding, and lost productivity. AI-driven predictive retention flags at-risk drivers 30–60 days before they quit.

These three combine to $150,000–$300,000/year for a typical 30–50 truck fleet.

## SLIDE 3 — THE DISPATCH AUTOMATION LAYER

**Headline:** Your dispatcher doesn't get replaced. They get 4× more capacity.

**What the AI dispatch layer does:**

1. Ingests all incoming load tenders (EDI, email, broker portals) automatically
2. Matches available drivers to loads based on HOS, location, endorsements, and preferences
3. Calculates route optimization across your fleet in real time
4. Generates driver assignments and check-call schedules automatically
5. Flags exceptions — overweight loads, HOS violations, weather delays — for human review

**Result at comparable fleets:**
- Dispatcher capacity: from 30 trucks managed → 75+ trucks managed
- Load acceptance rate: +18% (faster response to broker tenders)
- Dispatch errors: -80% (human fatigue removed from repetitive tasks)

Your dispatcher focuses on exceptions and relationships. AI handles the clock-in-clock-out.

## SLIDE 4 — EMPTY MILES REDUCTION

**Headline:** Every empty mile is margin you're handing back.

**How AI load matching works:**

- Monitors DAT, Truckstop, and your broker network in real time
- When a delivery is confirmed, AI immediately searches for return loads within delivery radius
- Matches based on driver HOS remaining, equipment type, lane profitability (not just gross rate)
- Books or flags the best option for dispatcher approval — all before the truck is unloaded

**Real numbers from comparable operations:**
- Deadhead rate: 21% → 13% (within 90 days of deployment)
- Revenue per truck per week: +$680 average (same drivers, same lanes)
- Fuel savings (avoided miles): 12,000–18,000 gallons/year at a 50-truck fleet

At current diesel prices, that's $50,000–$75,000 in fuel savings. Before touching rate negotiations.

## SLIDE 5 — DRIVER RETENTION AI

**Headline:** The $8,000 problem you can see coming 45 days out.

**What the retention model tracks:**

- Miles driven vs. driver preference (home time patterns, preferred lanes)
- Pay per mile trend over 30/60/90 days
- Load rejection rate (early indicator of dissatisfaction)
- Communication patterns with dispatch
- HOS utilization (underutilization = driver looking for more work elsewhere)

**When a driver scores "at risk":**
- Fleet manager gets an alert with the driver's specific friction point
- Suggested intervention: load preference adjustment, home time conversation, pay review
- 45 days of lead time vs. getting a two-weeks notice surprise

**ROI:** Reducing turnover from 78% to 50% at a 50-driver fleet saves $140,000–$280,000/year in replacement costs.
