### The Problem Michigan Contractors Actually Have

Before talking about AI, let's be specific about the cost.

**The estimating math nobody talks about:**

A competitive Michigan GC submits 40–60 bids per year. Each bid takes 12–20 hours of skilled estimator time — takeoffs, subcontractor coordination, scope writing, pricing.

At $35–$50/hour for an experienced estimator:
- 50 bids × 15 hours = 750 hours/year
- 750 hours × $42/hour = **$31,500/year in estimating labor**
- Win rate: typically 20–30% on competitive bids
- Cost per won project in estimating labor alone: **$10,500–$15,750**

That's before you account for the bids you lose because you couldn't respond fast enough. Speed is a competitive advantage in Michigan commercial construction — owners and GCs who can turn around a credible bid in 48–72 hours win work that slower competitors never even see.

**The change order problem:**

The second major cost: change orders that don't get captured. Industry research consistently shows that 15–25% of legitimate change order value goes uncaptured — either because the documentation process is too slow, the conversation was verbal-only, or the project team didn't know they could bill for it.

On a $1.5M project, that's $225,000–$375,000 in work you did but didn't get paid for.

These are the two places AI makes a measurable difference: **estimating speed** and **change order capture rate**.

### What AI Actually Does in Construction (And What It Doesn't)

AI doesn't replace experienced estimators or project managers. What it does: it takes the parts of their job that are time-consuming but not judgment-intensive, and handles those automatically.

**What AI handles well in construction:**

**1. Takeoff and quantity extraction from PDFs**

Modern AI (Claude Opus 4.8, GPT-4o) can read architectural and structural drawings, extract quantities, and pre-populate estimate templates with material counts. Not perfectly — but accurately enough that an estimator reviews a pre-populated estimate rather than building from scratch.

Time from: 4 hours of manual takeoff → 45 minutes of AI-assisted review.

**2. Subcontractor bid package assembly**

Pulling the right spec sections, scope documents, and drawing sets for each subcontractor used to take an estimator 2–3 hours per trade. AI can assemble these packages from a central document library in minutes, with the right pages flagged for each sub.

**3. Historical pricing lookup and escalation**

For materials your company has bought before, AI can pull historical pricing, apply escalation factors, and flag when current bids are out of range — before you submit.

**4. Change order documentation**

When a field super or PM notes a verbal change, AI can convert a quick voice memo or text message into a formatted RFI/change order draft with relevant spec cross-references — ready to send within minutes of the conversation.

**5. Subcontractor follow-up sequences**

The back-and-forth to collect sub bids is manual, repetitive, and time-consuming. An AI-driven sequence sends reminders, tracks who's responded, and flags gaps — without your estimator manually chasing 12 subs per bid.

### What AI Doesn't Do (Yet)

Be honest with yourself and your team about this:

- AI cannot replace the estimator's judgment on project risk, site conditions, or client relationship factors
- AI cannot reliably read low-quality scans or poorly organized drawing sets
- AI cannot assess a subcontractor's reliability or your relationship with a supplier

The best implementations pair AI's processing speed with experienced human judgment on the decisions that actually matter.

### The Michigan Construction Market: Why Now

Michigan's commercial construction market is recovering from supply chain disruptions and navigating a significant skilled labor shortage. The firms winning more work right now share three traits:

1. **They bid faster.** Speed of response is a differentiator when an owner needs a GC who's serious.
2. **They have tighter margins.** Labor costs are up 18–23% since 2020. Every estimating inefficiency hits the bottom line harder.
3. **They're holding onto subcontractor relationships.** The subs are busier than ever — GCs who make it easy to work with them (organized bid packages, fast responses, clear scope) get priority.

AI addresses all three.

### The Grant Angle: Why Michigan Contractors Are Paying Less Than You'd Expect

Two programs overlap for Michigan construction firms:

**Michigan Going PRO Talent Fund**

Covers AI training costs for Michigan employers. Up to $50,000 per employer per year. The training component of any AI deployment — onboarding your estimating team, teaching your PMs the change order workflow — qualifies.

**Michigan Industry 4.0 Technology Grant**

Explicitly covers manufacturers and construction firms deploying automation and AI. 50% reimbursement on implementation costs for small manufacturers and small contractors. This grant is significantly underutilized in construction because most contractors don't know it applies to them.

A $20,000 AI estimating and change order system for a Michigan GC:
