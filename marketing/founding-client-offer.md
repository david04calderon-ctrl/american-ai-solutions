# Founding Client Offer
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** The first client is the hardest because you have no case studies, no references, and no production track record. The founding client offer solves that. You trade margin for speed — a 50% rate reduction in exchange for a case study, a reference call, and a LinkedIn recommendation. One founding client opens every conversation that follows.
**The math:** $7,500 today + case study = worth $75,000+ in sales credibility over the next 12 months.
**When to use:** First 60–90 days in business, or when entering a new vertical with no vertical-specific case study.
**Limit:** 1 founding client per vertical (4 total across all verticals). Never more than 1 active at a time — you can't run 4 discounted projects simultaneously.

---

## THE OFFER

**What you're offering:**
- Full AI implementation (same scope, same quality, same deliverables as any other project)
- At 50% of standard project rate
- Example: $15K standard → $7,500 founding client rate
- Example: $20K standard → $10,000 founding client rate

**What you're asking in return:**
1. A completed case study (your draft, their review, published with their approval)
2. Available as a reference call for future prospects (up to 3 calls per year, 15 minutes each)
3. A LinkedIn recommendation (short, in their voice) posted on David's profile

**This is not charity.** The founding client gets a world-class AI implementation for half price. You get the social proof that makes every future sale easier. Both parties win.

---

## WHO QUALIFIES AS A FOUNDING CLIENT

**Yes:**
- Business that fits your ICP exactly (right vertical, right size, right problem)
- Decision-maker is willing to sign before you have case studies in their vertical
- Operation is clean enough to implement in — not mid-crisis, not restructuring
- Willing to be identified (case study requires at least company name, ideally their name and title)
- Can serve as a credible reference (their peers would recognize and respect them)

**No:**
- Anyone who seems like they'll be difficult — the discount doesn't make a bad client tolerable
- Anyone who needs extreme hand-holding beyond the scope
- Anyone outside your target geographies
- Anyone who haggles on the discounted price — if they negotiate a 50% discount, that's a character signal

### Target selection (before reaching out)

Identify 3–5 ideal founding client candidates per vertical:
- Companies you'd want as a case study — credible, recognizable enough to name in outreach
- Decision-makers who are accessible (mutual LinkedIn connections, warm intro possible, or strong cold fit)
- Operation that matches one of the 4–5 use cases in michigan-ai-use-case-library.md perfectly

## THE FOUNDING CLIENT CONVERSATION

When they respond positively and book a call, the conversation follows the discovery call framework (see discovery-call-framework.md) — but with one addition.

**Near the end of the call, after you've identified the problem and fit:**

> "Based on what you've described, I think we're looking at [specific use case] — and this is exactly the kind of project I'd want to showcase as a Michigan [vertical] case study.
>
> Here's how I want to structure this: I'll give you the full implementation — same scope, same quality, same weekly updates, same results. At half my standard rate, which is $[founding client price].
>
> In exchange, at the 90-day mark when the results are visible, I'd like to write a case study. I'll draft it based on the results — you review, edit to your voice, and approve before anything is published. Nothing goes out without your sign-off.
>
> I'd also ask you to be available for a brief reference call if future clients ask to speak with a Michigan [vertical] that I've worked with. Maybe 3 calls per year, 15 minutes each — just your honest experience.
>
> Does that sound fair?"

**Then stop talking.**

Most qualified prospects respond one of three ways:
1. "Yes, that's fair — let's talk next steps." → Move to proposal.
2. "Can I think about it?" → "Of course. I'll send you the project outline and the rate in writing. Take whatever time you need." → Follow up per proposal-follow-up-system.md.
3. "Why do you need a case study?" → "I want other Michigan [vertical] clients to see this kind of result from a Michigan firm. Proof of work in this market matters, and I want to build it honestly."

## THE CASE STUDY CAPTURE PROCESS

Reference case-study-template.md for the full case study structure. For founding client specifically:

**Day 1 (project start):**
- Document all baseline metrics (see client-success-metrics.md Day 1 baseline)
- Confirm case study permission in writing — include in the SOW as a project deliverable

**Day 90 (results call):**
- Pull baseline vs. current metrics
- Translate to dollars
- Record the results call (with permission) — Fathom transcribes, you pull the best quotes

**Within 72 hours of results call:**
- Write the case study draft using case-study-template.md
- Send to client: "Here's the draft — please edit anything that doesn't sound right or that you'd prefer not to share. Once you approve it, we'll publish."

**After client approval:**
- Publish on the website (blog section or dedicated case studies page)
- Post to LinkedIn with client tagged (if they're comfortable)
- Add to the proposal template as supporting evidence
- Use in email outreach: "I recently completed [anonymized version] for a Michigan [vertical]..."

## WHAT TO DO AFTER THE FIRST FOUNDING CLIENT

**Immediately:**
- Update your Calendly intro: "I've worked with Michigan [vertical] clients on [use case]"
- Update cold email sequences: "I recently completed a [use case] for a Michigan [vertical] — results: [numbers]"
- Update the discovery call opening: "Before we dig in — I've recently implemented this kind of system for a Michigan [their type of business], so I can speak to what actually works in your context"
- Update your LinkedIn headline: include "[vertical] AI implementation" as a credential

**Cold outreach upgrade:**
The first case study unlocks a new first-line variation in your email sequence:

> "Just completed an AI quality reporting automation for a [Michigan automotive supplier / similar company] — they went from 22 hours a week of manual reporting to 6. I'm reaching out to [Company] because your operation sounds similar."

This one line changes the game. It's proof, not claims.

---

## THE FOUNDING CLIENT LIMIT

Once you have a case study in a vertical, **stop offering the founding client rate in that vertical.** The discount was a trade — you got what you needed (the case study). Now price full.

If someone asks for a discount in a vertical where you have a case study:
> "I offered a founding client rate early on because I was building the Michigan [vertical] proof of work. I have that now — I'd rather compete on the results than the price."

Then use the objection-pricing-guide.md for any remaining price pressure.
