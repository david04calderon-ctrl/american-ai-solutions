### Post O-01 — When AI Is the Wrong Answer

```
I turn down business.

Not often. But sometimes a prospect describes their problem and the
honest answer is: AI is not what you need right now.

Here's what that looks like:

→ Your data is too messy to build on. If your ERP has 5 years of
  inconsistent entries and nobody owns the data quality, we're going
  to spend 80% of the project cleaning data and 20% building AI.
  Fix the data first.

→ Your process doesn't exist yet. AI automates what you already do.
  If your team hasn't agreed on a standard process, automating it
  makes a mess faster.

→ Your team is at 10% capacity. If you're in a restructuring or a
  leadership crisis, this isn't the moment for a technology project.

→ The ROI doesn't clear the bar. Some operations are already
  efficient enough that the AI investment doesn't make economic
  sense at their scale.

I tell prospects when they're in one of these situations. They almost
always appreciate it. Some of them come back 6 months later when
the situation has changed.

Being honest about what doesn't work is how you build the credibility
to be trusted about what does.
```
