**Impact:** Every prospect, partner, and referral source who receives an email sees the brand, the differentiation, and a CTA.

## GMAIL SETUP — STEP BY STEP

### Creating multiple signatures:

1. Gmail → Settings (gear icon) → See all settings → Signature tab
2. Click "+ Create new"
3. Name: "Full — First Contact"
4. Paste Version 1 content
5. Click "+ Create new" again
6. Name: "Short — Replies"
7. Paste Version 2 content

### Setting defaults:
- For new emails: "Full — First Contact"
- For replies/forwards: "Short — Replies"

### HTML formatting tips for Gmail:
- **Bold** the name and company name (select text, click B)
- Make the Calendly link a hyperlink (select text, click the link icon, paste URL)
- Keep font: Sans-serif, 12–13px — don't use exotic fonts that render differently on other clients
- **No images in the signature** — images are blocked by many corporate email clients and increase spam scores
- Use separator characters (| · —) instead of images for visual structure

## WHAT THE SIGNATURE DOES (AND WHY IT'S DESIGNED THIS WAY)

**"Michigan Going PRO Grant Expert"** — This line does two things:
1. Differentiates from every other AI consultant (most don't know anything about Going PRO)
2. Creates a question in the reader's mind: "What's this grant? Does my project qualify?" — which is a reason to click or reply

**The Calendly link in the signature** — Every prospect who gets a reply from David has a zero-friction path to booking a call. No "let me know your availability" back-and-forth. Just a link.

**"Michigan manufacturers · Healthcare Practices · Fleets · Contractors"** — Lets every reader self-identify. A healthcare practice manager sees themselves. A fleet operator sees themselves. This increases click-through on the website link.

**Two-line short signature for replies** — Respects the reader's time. Nobody needs to see a 10-line signature on every reply in a thread. The short version keeps it clean while still having the Calendly link.

## EMAIL FOOTER FOR NEWSLETTERS / DRIP SEQUENCES

For any email that goes out through HubSpot sequences or newsletters, add a legal footer:

```
You're receiving this because [reason — e.g., "we connected on LinkedIn" or "you requested information about AI automation"]. 
To unsubscribe: [unsubscribe link]

American AI Solutions LLC | Detroit, Michigan | david@americanaisolutionsllc.com
```

This is required under CAN-SPAM (US law) for commercial email. HubSpot handles the unsubscribe link automatically when using sequences.
