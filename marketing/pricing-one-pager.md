### The three ways we work

**1. AI Workflow Audit — $2,500**
*Delivered in 48–72 hours*

A focused analysis of one operational area in your business: where AI can move the needle, what it would take to build, and what the ROI looks like. We deliver a written findings report and a prioritized roadmap — not a sales pitch, a real analysis.

Most clients use the Audit to answer: *"Is this worth a bigger investment?"* In our experience, 70%+ of clients who do an Audit move forward with a Quick Deploy.

The $2,500 Audit cost applies to any Quick Deploy project signed within 60 days.

---

**2. Quick Deploy — $7,500–$18,000**
*Delivered in 3–6 weeks*

A production-ready AI system targeting one high-ROI workflow in your business. Examples:

- Prior authorization automation for a healthcare practice
- Quality inspection and defect tracking for a manufacturing line
- Dispatch and load optimization layer for a trucking fleet
- Estimating and change order automation for a contractor

What's included: system design, build, integration with your existing tools, staff training, documentation (runbook + Loom walkthroughs), and 30 days of post-launch support.

**Michigan businesses:** Every Quick Deploy includes a Going PRO grant application package. Most clients receive $3,000–$8,000 in training cost reimbursement. We handle the paperwork.

---

**3. Full Implementation — $20,000–$45,000**
*Delivered in 8–14 weeks*

A multi-workflow AI deployment that connects multiple systems and automates several processes across your operation. For manufacturers: quality + production monitoring + exception alerting. For healthcare groups: prior auth + patient communication + documentation. For fleet operators: load optimization + driver retention + dispatch automation.

Includes all Quick Deploy deliverables plus system architecture documentation, integration with multiple platforms, and 90 days of support.

### How Michigan grant funding changes the math

Michigan has two programs that directly apply to AI deployments at Michigan businesses:

**Going PRO Talent Fund** — Covers training costs for AI implementations. $2,000 per trained employee ($3,500 per USDOL Registered Apprentice). Accessible to manufacturers, healthcare, logistics, and construction businesses.

**Michigan Industry 4.0 Technology Grant** — For small manufacturers (NAICS 31–33) implementing advanced manufacturing technology. Up to $25,000 (50% reimbursement). Competitive, but accessible.

On a $15,000 project, grant funding commonly offsets $3,000–$8,000 of the cost. We include grant application documentation in every engagement — you don't need to figure it out separately.
