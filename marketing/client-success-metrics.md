# Client Success Metrics Tracker
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** Every client engagement needs measurable proof it worked. This tracker defines the exact KPIs to capture before, during, and after each project — by vertical. Numbers close deals (testimonials, case studies, referrals). No numbers = no proof = no momentum.
**When to use:** Start collecting baseline metrics on Day 1 of every project. Review at 30 days. Show results at 90 days.
**The rule:** Before you build anything, you need to know what you're measuring. Before you leave, you need to know what changed.

---

## THE BASELINE CAPTURE — DO THIS ON DAY 1

Before writing a single line of code or building a single workflow, capture these numbers from the client. Ask directly. If they don't know the exact number, estimate together.

**Universal baseline questions (all verticals):**

1. How many hours per week does your team spend on [the process we're automating]?
2. How many people are involved in that process?
3. What does that cost you at loaded labor rate? *(if they don't know: hours × $35–$55/hr)*
4. How often does an error occur in this process? What does an error cost?
5. How long does the process take from start to finish (cycle time)?
6. What's the bottleneck — the slowest step in the current process?

**Log all baselines in HubSpot** as deal properties. Date-stamp them. Screenshot or export any dashboards they share with you.

## AUTOMOTIVE TIER 2/3 SUPPLIERS

### Quality Inspection / OEE Dashboard

**Baseline metrics to capture (Day 1):**

| Metric | How to measure | Baseline target |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Current OEE % | From production logs or MES if available; estimate if not | Typical 55–70% |
| Hours/week on quality reporting | Ask the quality manager | Usually 15–30 hrs/week |
| Time to answer an OEM data request | Ask how long it takes to pull audit-ready data | Typical 1–4 hours |
| Defect escape rate (PPM) | From scrap/rework logs | Varies by process |
| Monthly scrap/rework cost | From accounting or production reports | Varies |
| Number of people involved in quality process | Count heads | 2–5 typical |

**30-Day metrics to check:**

- OEE trend (is it moving up, even 1–2 points?)
- Time to answer OEM data request (should be <15 minutes with dashboard live)
- Reduction in quality reporting hours (target: 50–70% reduction)
- Any defect catches the old process would have missed

**90-Day metrics to present:**

| Metric | Baseline | 90-Day Result | Change |
|--------|----------|---------------|--------|
| OEE % | [X]% | [Y]% | +[Z] points |
| Quality reporting hours/week | [X] hrs | [Y] hrs | -[Z] hrs |
| Data request response time | [X] hours | [Y] minutes | -[Z]% |
| Defect escape rate (PPM) | [X] | [Y] | -[Z]% |

**Translate to dollars at 90-day review:**
- Hours saved × $45/hr loaded rate = $[X]/year labor savings
- OEE improvement × line throughput × margin = $[X]/year revenue recapture
- Scrap reduction × scrap cost per unit = $[X]/year direct savings

### Prior Authorization Automation

**Baseline metrics to capture (Day 1):**

| Metric | How to measure | Baseline target |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Prior auths submitted per week | From billing team | Varies by practice size |
| Hours/week on prior auth | Ask billing coordinator | Typical 15–40 hrs/week |
| Prior auth denial rate | Ask — or estimate from billing data | Industry avg: 7–14% |
| Average days to auth approval | Track a sample of recent auths | Typical 3–7 days |

- Denial rate on AI-assisted auths (track separately from manual)

| Metric | Baseline | 90-Day Result | Change |

| Days to approval | [X] days | [Y] days | -[Z] days |
| Revenue cleared from hold | — | $[X]/month | New |

**Translate to dollars:**
- Hours saved × $28/hr (billing coordinator rate) = $[X]/year

## MICHIGAN FLEET OPERATORS (LOGISTICS/TRUCKING)

### Dispatch Optimization

**Baseline metrics to capture (Day 1):**

| Metric | How to measure | Baseline target |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Deadhead percentage | Total deadhead miles ÷ total miles × 100 | Industry avg: 20–28% |
| Trucks per dispatcher | Count | Typical 25–35 |
| Average load match time | How long to find a backhaul after delivery | Typical 45–120 min |
| Empty miles per truck per week | From TMS or fuel cards | Varies |
| Revenue per truck per week | From TMS | Varies by lane |
| Driver detention hours per week | From TMS logs | Varies |

**Calculate the annual deadhead cost at baseline:**
- Total miles/year × deadhead % × cost per mile ($1.20–$1.50 loaded equivalent) = $[X]/year lost to deadhead

**30-Day metrics:**

- Deadhead percentage (week-over-week trend)
- Load match time (should drop meaningfully by week 3–4)
- Dispatcher workload (are they handling more trucks?)

**90-Day metrics to present:**

| Metric | Baseline | 90-Day Result | Change |
|--------|----------|---------------|--------|
| Deadhead % | [X]% | [Y]% | -[Z] points |
| Trucks per dispatcher | [X] | [Y] | +[Z] trucks |
| Load match time | [X] min | [Y] min | -[Z]% |
| Revenue per truck/week | $[X] | $[Y] | +[Z]% |

**Translate to dollars:**
- Deadhead reduction × total miles × $1.20/mile = $[X]/year direct savings
- Dispatcher efficiency gain → avoid [X] headcount addition at $55K/year = $[X] avoided cost
- Revenue per truck improvement × fleet size × 52 weeks = $[X]/year top-line

## THE 90-DAY RESULTS PRESENTATION TEMPLATE

Use this structure for every 90-day check-in call (30 minutes):

**Agenda:**

1. **Opening (2 min):** "We're 90 days in. I want to show you what the data says — and then I want to hear from you."

2. **What we measured at Day 1 (3 min):** Show the baseline numbers you captured together. Remind them of the starting point.

3. **What changed (10 min):** Go through the 90-day metrics table. For each metric, give the number and translate it to dollars. Let silence work — don't rush past results.

4. **Total impact summary (3 min):**
   > "In 90 days, the system has recovered approximately $[X] in [labor / revenue / cost avoidance]. Annualized, that's $[Y]. Your total investment in this project was $[Z]. You're at [X]× return in 90 days."

5. **What's next (10 min):** "Now that we've proven this works — let me show you where the next opportunity is." (This is where you introduce Year 2 expansion from the expansion playbook.)

6. **Ask (2 min):** "Would it be okay if I used these numbers in a case study? I'd keep it anonymous if you prefer — just the vertical and the results."

## THE CASE STUDY CAPTURE PROCESS

At the 90-day call, ask for permission to document results. Most clients say yes.

**What to capture:**
- Vertical and company size (can be anonymous: "Michigan Tier 2 automotive supplier, 85 employees")
- The before-state (what the pain looked like)
- The system we built (one paragraph, no jargon)
- The results (the 90-day metrics table above)
- A quote from the client (ideally on the phone — "Can I quote you saying that?")

**How to use it:**
- Case study section on website (americanaisolutionsllc.com/results/)
- PDF to send during proposal stage
- LinkedIn post with client permission
- Carousel slide: before/after stat card
- Testimonial section of proposals

**Anonymization rules:**
- Always get explicit permission before using company name
- Offer anonymized version first: "I'd like to write this up as a case study — we can keep your company name anonymous if you prefer, just describe you as a Michigan [vertical] company. Would that be okay?"
- Store permission status in HubSpot contact record

## METRICS STORAGE IN HUBSPOT

Create a custom Deal property group: **"Project Metrics"**

Properties to add:
- Baseline metric 1 (text): e.g., "22 hrs/week on prior auth"
- Baseline metric 2 (text)
- Baseline metric 3 (text)
- 90-day result 1 (text): e.g., "4 hrs/week on prior auth"
- 90-day result 2 (text)
- 90-day result 3 (text)
- Dollar value of results (currency): Total estimated annual impact
- Case study permission (checkbox): Client approved case study use
- Anonymized (checkbox): Case study is anonymous

These properties make it easy to filter for case study candidates and to pull aggregate data on ROI delivered across all clients.
