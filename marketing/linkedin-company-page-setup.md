# LinkedIn Company Page Setup Guide
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** A LinkedIn Company Page for American AI Solutions LLC does four things a personal profile cannot: (1) it shows up in Google searches for "American AI Solutions LLC", (2) it lets you publish content that lives on the brand page separately from David's personal feed, (3) it gives credibility to prospects who check the company before the call, and (4) it allows future employees and partners to be associated with the company.
**Time to set up:** 45 minutes.
**Priority:** Set this up the same week you optimize your personal profile.

---

## WHY BOTH PAGES MATTER

**Personal profile:** Your primary growth engine. Where content gets reach. Where connection requests come from. Where discovery call bookings originate.

**Company page:** Legitimacy signal. Google indexes it. Prospects who receive a proposal will search "American AI Solutions LLC" — the company page should be the first result, right. It also shows up on your personal profile under your Experience section — clicking your company name there takes prospects to the company page. If there's no company page, that link goes nowhere.

---

## SECTION 1: CREATE THE PAGE

1. Go to linkedin.com/company/setup/new
2. Select: Company
3. Company name: **American AI Solutions LLC**
4. LinkedIn public URL: Set to **linkedin.com/company/american-ai-solutions** (or american-ai-solutions-llc if taken)
5. Website: americanaisolutionsllc.com
6. Industry: Information Technology & Services
7. Company size: 1–10 employees (correct and honest)
8. Company type: Privately Held
9. Click "Create page"

## SECTION 6: CONNECTING YOUR PERSONAL PROFILE

When you edit the "American AI Solutions LLC" entry in your personal profile's Experience section, LinkedIn will search for a matching company page. Select the company page you just created.

This creates a two-way link:
- Your personal profile → click company name → goes to company page
- Company page → list of employees → shows you as founder

This signals to prospects that the company is real. A personal profile with no linked company page looks like a freelancer. A linked company page looks like a business.

## SECTION 7: CONTENT STRATEGY FOR THE COMPANY PAGE

The company page gets less organic reach than your personal profile — LinkedIn deprioritizes company page content in feeds. Use it strategically, not as your primary content channel.

**What to post on the company page:**
1. **Reshares of your personal posts** — 2–3 times per week. Click "Share" on your personal post → share to company page. This shows the company page is active.
2. **Milestone announcements** — "AAIS is live on the web. americanaisolutionsllc.com is now published." "First client project complete." These live better on the company page than the personal profile.
3. **Blog post links** — When a new blog post publishes, share it from the company page: "[Title] — our new guide for Michigan [vertical] businesses. Link in first comment."
4. **Industry news with commentary** — When Michigan manufacturing news happens (OEM announcement, grant update), share it with one paragraph of commentary.

**Post frequency on company page:** 3–5 times/week (mostly reshares)
**Post frequency on personal profile:** 3–5 times/week (original content)

## SECTION 8: LINKEDIN ADS (When Ready)

When you have 3–5 case studies and a content baseline, LinkedIn Ads are the highest-quality B2B ad channel in Michigan. The targeting precision is unmatched.

**Targeting options for AAIS:**
- Job titles: Plant Manager, Quality Manager, Operations Director, Practice Manager, Fleet Manager, VP Operations
- Company size: 10–500 employees
- Geography: Michigan (state-level targeting)
- Industry: Automotive, Health Care, Transportation/Trucking, Construction

**Ad types that work for AAIS:**
- **Single image ad:** "Your Michigan quality dashboard in 30 days" → Calendly link
- **Document ad:** The AI tools comparison guide (ai-tools-client-guide.md) as a lead magnet
- **Thought leader ad:** Boost your best personal post as a sponsored ad

**Minimum budget to test:** $500–$1,000/month for 30 days. At CPL (cost per lead) of $50–$150, expect 5–15 leads from $500 spend.

**Don't run ads until:** You have a working website, a live blog with 3+ posts, a case study, and you're converting organic leads. Ads amplify what's already working — they don't fix a broken funnel.

## SECTION 9: LINKEDIN ANALYTICS

Once the page has 30 days of activity, check:
- **Followers:** Growing? (Target: 50 in Month 1, 200 by Month 3)
- **Visitor analytics:** Who's viewing the page? (Job titles, companies — this tells you if the right audience is finding you)
- **Content analytics:** Which posts from personal that you reshared got the most reach?
- **Employee count:** Just you for now — add contractors or future hires when appropriate
