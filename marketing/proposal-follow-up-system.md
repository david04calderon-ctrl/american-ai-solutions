# Proposal Follow-Up System
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** The systematic process for every proposal sent — from the moment you hit send through signature. Most deals die in the follow-up gap, not in the meeting. This is how you close.
**The rule:** You follow up until you get a yes, a no, or a clear "not now." "I haven't read it yet" is not a no. Silence is not a no. Only a clear signal ends the sequence.

---

## THE PROPOSAL FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

### Day 0 — Send the Proposal

**Immediately after sending:**
- Send via Pandadoc (open tracking is ON — you'll see when they view it)
- Send a short email with the proposal attached or linked:

> Subject: [Company Name] — AI System Proposal + Grant Math
>
> [Name] —
>
> Proposal attached. The key numbers are on pages 2–3 — specifically the net cost after Michigan grant reimbursement.
>
> I'm available for a call this week if you want to walk through any section together. Otherwise, I'll follow up Thursday.
>
> David

**Also:**
- Log "Proposal Sent" in HubSpot — set next follow-up task for Day 3
- Note in HubSpot: what was discussed on the discovery call, what mattered to them most

---

### Day 3 — The Value-Add Follow-Up

**Trigger:** 3 days after sending, regardless of whether they've opened it (Pandadoc tells you)

**If they HAVE opened the proposal:**
> Subject: Re: [Company Name] — AI System Proposal
>
> [Name] —
>
> Checking in to see if the proposal landed the way I intended.
>
> One thing I wanted to add: the Going PRO grant application for Q[X] closes [date]. If we start the project before then, I can include the training component in this application cycle — which typically moves the net cost from $[X] to $[X-grant].
>
> Happy to schedule a 20-minute call to walk through any questions.
>
> David

**If they have NOT opened the proposal:**
> Subject: Quick note — [Company Name] Proposal
>
> [Name] —
>
> Wanted to make sure the proposal came through — sometimes these land in spam.
>
> I'll send the direct link here as well: [Pandadoc link]
>
> The grant section (page 3) is the part most people want to review first — it explains the net cost after Michigan reimbursement.
>
> Let me know if you have any questions.

---

### Day 7 — The Friction-Removal Follow-Up

**Trigger:** 7 days after proposal sent

**Goal:** Find out what's blocking them and remove the obstacle

> Subject: [Company Name] — any questions on the proposal?
>
> [Name] —
>
> Following up on the proposal. A few things I sometimes hear at this stage:
>
> "The budget isn't confirmed yet." → The grant math changes the effective cost significantly — happy to walk through that.
>
> "I need to discuss with my partner/COO." → I can do a 20-minute call with both of you, or I can prepare a one-page summary tailored for that conversation.
>
> "I want to think about it more." → Completely fair. What specific questions are still open for you?
>
> Let me know what's on your mind — I'd rather address it directly than have it sit unanswered.
>
> David

---

### Day 14 — The Decision Check-In

**Trigger:** 2 weeks after proposal sent

> Subject: [Company Name] — following up
>
> [Name] —
>
> Two weeks since I sent the proposal. I want to be respectful of your time and not spam your inbox, but I also want to make sure I'm not dropping the ball on my end.
>
> Three possible answers that are all fine:
> 1. "We want to move forward" — great, I'll send the agreement
> 2. "We need more time" — fine, when should I follow up?
> 3. "It's not the right fit or timing" — I appreciate the honesty and I'll close out my end
>
> Where are you?
>
> David

*(This "three possible answers" structure is disarming — it explicitly gives them permission to say no, which makes it easier for them to say yes.)*

---

### Day 21 — The Loom Follow-Up

**Trigger:** 3 weeks after proposal sent, if still no response

Record a 2-minute Loom:
- Show the proposal on screen
- Walk through the 3 most important numbers (project cost, grant reimbursement, net cost)
- End with: "Happy to do a quick call to answer any questions. Link is below."

> Subject: [Company Name] — 2-minute video walk-through of the proposal
>
> [Name] —
>
> Recorded a quick 2-minute video walking through the key numbers from your proposal, since sometimes it's easier to hear than read.
>
> [Loom link]
>
> If you want to talk through it live, the calendar link is below.
>
> David

---

### Day 30 — The Final Follow-Up

**Trigger:** 30 days after proposal sent with no response

> Subject: [Company Name] — closing out on my end
>
> [Name] —
>
> I've been following up for a month on the proposal and I don't want to keep filling your inbox if the timing or fit isn't right.
>
> I'm going to close this out on my end — but I'll leave the proposal link active. If things change in the next quarter, reach out and we'll pick up where we left off.
>
> One thing I'll mention before I go: the Q3 Michigan Going PRO grant cycle closes October 1. If timing is the issue, that's worth knowing.
>
> Good luck with the operation — I hope the numbers work out for you.
>
> David Calderon
> American AI Solutions LLC

*(This final email often generates responses — the prospect realizes they've been meaning to reply and the "I'm closing this out" framing creates urgency. It also maintains the relationship without burning it.)*

## PANDADOC SETUP — CRITICAL

Pandadoc gives you open tracking, signature requests, and templating. This is how you know when to call vs. when to email.

**What the data tells you:**
- **Opened + 3+ views:** They're seriously considering — call them. Today.
- **Opened once, no further views:** They looked but didn't engage — follow up with value add
- **Not opened:** The email may have gone to spam, or they're overwhelmed — try different subject line or phone call
- **Signed:** Call immediately to celebrate and set kickoff call

**Pandadoc alert setup:**
- Go to Pandadoc → Settings → Notifications → Email me when a document is viewed
- Also set a webhook to HubSpot (if connected) to log views automatically

**After they sign:**
- Immediately send the kickoff email (template in client-onboarding-sop.md)
- Invoice for 50% deposit (Stripe or Pandadoc payments)
- Book the kickoff call within 48 hours

## NO-SHOW RECOVERY — For Missed Discovery Calls

Before you even send a proposal, you'll have prospects who book calls and don't show. Here's the recovery sequence:

### Immediately (within 15 minutes of no-show):

> Subject: Missed you at [time] today
>
> [Name] —
>
> We had a call today at [time] — just wanted to make sure you're okay.
>
> Happy to reschedule whenever works for you. Here's the same booking link: [Calendly link]
>
> David

*(Keep it short and empathetic — don't assume bad faith. Life happens.)*

---

### 24 Hours Later (if no response):

> Subject: Rescheduling our call — [Company Name]
>
> [Name] —
>
> Following up on yesterday's missed call. I'd still like to connect — I looked at what I know about [Company Name]'s operation and I have some specific observations I wanted to share.
>
> If this week doesn't work, what's the best time in the next 2 weeks? Or you can book directly: [Calendly link]
>
> David

---

### Day 7 After No-Show (if still no response):

> Subject: [Company Name] — still interested in a call?
>
> [Name] —
>
> I don't want to overload your inbox. Quick yes or no: is there still interest in a conversation about AI automation for [their vertical]?
>
> If yes — here's the booking link: [Calendly]
> If no — completely fine. Just let me know and I'll stop following up.
>
> David

*(A direct yes/no question is harder to ignore than an open-ended one.)*

## STALLED DEAL RECOVERY — Prospects Who Were Warm Then Went Cold

Every pipeline has deals that were moving and stopped. Here's how to re-engage without looking desperate.

**The "relevant news" re-engage:**
When something relevant happens (new OEM announcement, Michigan grant news, industry development), use it as a natural reason to reach out:

> Subject: Relevant to our conversation — [news item]
>
> [Name] —
>
> Saw this and thought of our conversation in [month]: [link to relevant news/article]
>
> [One sentence on why it's relevant to them specifically]
>
> If the timing is better now, I'd still like to connect. The proposal is still on the table.
>
> David

**The "check in" re-engage (3 months after last contact):**
> Subject: [Company Name] — checking in
>
> [Name] —
>
> It's been a few months since we talked. Wanted to check in — how are things going with [specific process they mentioned]?
>
> No agenda — just genuine curiosity. If anything's changed and the AI project makes more sense now, I'm still here.
>
> David

## DEAL STAGE TRACKING IN HUBSPOT

Every proposal in the HubSpot pipeline should move through these stages:

| Stage | Definition | Next action |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Qualified | Discovery call done, good fit | Send proposal within 48 hours |
| Proposal Sent | Pandadoc sent, Day 0 | Day 3 follow-up |
| Proposal Opened | They viewed the doc | Call today |
| Negotiating | Responding, discussing terms | Move fast — momentum is everything |
| Verbal Yes | They said yes, not signed | Send agreement within 24 hours |
| Contract Sent | Pandadoc agreement out | Follow up daily until signed |
| Won | Signed + deposit paid | Kickoff within 48 hours |
| Lost | Clear no | Note reason, add to 90-day re-engage queue |
| Nurture | Not now, future potential | 90-day newsletter/touchpoint cadence |

**Deal properties to track:**
- Proposal amount (total contract value)
- Grant-adjusted net cost
- Vertical (automotive, healthcare, logistics, construction)
- Primary objection (if lost)
- Source (cold email, LinkedIn, referral, inbound)

## CLOSE RATE BENCHMARKS

**What to aim for:**
- Discovery call → Proposal sent: 60%+
- Proposal sent → Close: 30%+
- Blended: ~18% of qualified discovery calls → closed client

**At 5 discovery calls/week:** 3 proposals, 1 close every 2 weeks = 2 closes/month
**At 10 discovery calls/week:** 6 proposals, 2 closes/week = 8 closes/month

If your close rate is below 20% on proposals, the issue is usually:
1. Qualifying too loosely (the wrong people are getting proposals)
2. The proposal isn't connecting grant math to their specific numbers
3. Follow-up is stopping too early (most deals close on touch 5–8, not touch 1–2)

Track every deal that doesn't close and note the primary reason. Patterns tell you where to fix the system.
