# LinkedIn Personal Profile Optimization Guide
# American AI Solutions LLC — David Calderon

**Purpose:** Your LinkedIn profile is the first thing every prospect checks before a discovery call. It's also how inbound leads find you. This guide covers every section, field, and strategy to turn your profile into a 24/7 lead qualification machine.
**Time to implement:** 90 minutes the first time. Updates take 5 minutes.
**Priority:** Do this before or immediately after posting your first carousel.

---

## THE HIERARCHY OF WHAT PEOPLE LOOK AT

When a prospect clicks your LinkedIn profile, they do this (in order):
1. **Profile photo** — Is this a real person I'd trust with my business?
2. **Headline** — What does this person do? Is it relevant to me?
3. **Banner image** — Does this look professional or did they leave the default?
4. **About section** — What's their pitch in their own words?
5. **Featured section** — What's their best work?
6. **Experience** — Is this legit? How long have they been doing this?
7. **Recommendations** — What do real people say?

Each of these is a conversion point. Let's optimize all of them.

## SECTION 1: PROFILE PHOTO

**What it needs to be:**
- Clear, well-lit headshot — head and shoulders, nothing below the chest
- Professional but not stiff — a slight smile, direct eye contact with the camera
- Solid or blurred background — a nice background if you have one (TechTown, Detroit cityscape), not a busy background
- Current — within 2 years

**What it must NOT be:**
- Casual photo from an event or family photo cropped out
- Low-resolution or pixelated
- Sunglasses or hat
- Group photo

**The technical spec:**
- 400×400 px minimum (LinkedIn displays at this size)
- JPG or PNG
- File size: under 8MB

**Quick fix if you don't have a good headshot:**
Use your phone. Good window light + a solid-color wall + someone holding the phone at eye level = a perfectly usable headshot. Don't let the absence of a "professional" photo stop you from having any photo.

**Profile photo border:**
LinkedIn lets you add a ring around your photo. Set it to your brand blue (#1A3A8F hex) if available, or leave it default. Don't use the green "open to work" ring — you're a founder, not a job seeker.

## SECTION 2: HEADLINE (220 characters max)

Your headline appears everywhere: search results, comment sections, connection requests, everywhere. It needs to communicate what you do in under 10 words.

**Current default (bad):** "Founder at American AI Solutions LLC"
*This tells people nothing about what you actually do.*

**Optimized version:**
```
AI Automation for Michigan Manufacturers, Healthcare Practices & Fleets | Detroit-based | Going PRO Grant Expert
```

**Why this works:**
- Names exactly who you work with (manufacturers, healthcare, fleets)
- Geographically specific (Michigan / Detroit)
- Includes a differentiator (Going PRO Grant Expert — most AI consultants don't say this)
- No jargon — "AI Automation" is clear

**Alternative (shorter, punchier):**
```
Building AI Systems for Michigan Business | Manufacturing · Healthcare · Logistics | Detroit
```

**Do NOT use:**
- "Passionate about AI" (meaningless)
- "Helping businesses leverage AI" (vague)
- "AI Consultant | Machine Learning | NLP | Data Science | Automation" (keyword stuffing)
- Job titles only — "CEO" or "Founder" (tells nobody what you do)

## SECTION 5: FEATURED SECTION

The Featured section lets you pin 3–5 items that appear prominently on your profile. This is where you put your best content.

**What to pin (in order of priority):**

**Pin 1: Your Loom intro video (or Calendly link)**
- Once recorded, share the Loom link as a post
- Feature that post → it shows the video thumbnail on your profile
- Click rate is significantly higher than static links

**Pin 2: Your best LinkedIn carousel (after you've posted it)**
- Feature the post → shows the first slide as a thumbnail
- This tells prospects exactly what vertical you serve and how you think

**Pin 3: Link to your blog or website**
- Use americanaisolutionsllc.com or /blog/
- In the description: "AI automation for Michigan businesses — what's actually working in 2026"

**How to add to Featured:**
1. Edit Profile → scroll to Featured → Add
2. You can add Posts (from your LinkedIn content), Links, or Media
3. For Calendly: Add → Link → paste your Calendly URL → write a description: "Book a free 30-minute AI strategy call"

## SECTION 10: NOTIFICATIONS AND SETTINGS

**Turn on Creator Mode:**
Profile → Resources → Creator Mode → On
This changes your "Connect" button to "Follow" — builds audience instead of limiting to 30,000 connections. Also gives you access to the content dashboard.

**LinkedIn newsletter:**
Once Creator Mode is on, you can start a LinkedIn newsletter. When someone subscribes, every issue goes to their email AND LinkedIn inbox. Consider launching "Michigan AI Weekly" — short, practical, Michigan-specific. Even 50 subscribers = warm list you didn't have before.

**Turn on "Open to" for services:**
Edit Profile → Open to → Providing services → Check all relevant categories
This adds a "Services" badge and makes you discoverable in LinkedIn's service provider search.

## LINKEDIN CONTENT STRATEGY TIE-IN

Your profile and your content work together. Every post builds the profile's credibility — and the profile converts the people who find you through posts.

**Sequence:**
1. Optimize profile (this guide) — Day 1
2. Post first carousel — Day 2
3. Send 15 connection requests per day with personalized notes — ongoing
4. Comment on 5 posts per day from Michigan business owners — ongoing
5. Reply to every comment on your posts within 2 hours — ongoing

At 3 posts/week + 15 connects/day + 5 comments/day:
- Week 4: 60 new connections, 3 posts, 100+ profile views
- Week 8: 200+ new connections, 6 posts reaching 5,000+ impressions
- Week 12: First inbound inquiry from LinkedIn content

The profile is what converts the curious into the booked.
