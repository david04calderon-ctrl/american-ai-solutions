# Discovery Call Framework — American AI Solutions LLC
**Call length:** 30 minutes
**Goal:** Understand the pain, score the fit, either book a follow-up or disqualify early
**Never:** Pitch on this call. Listen 80%, talk 20%.
**Tool:** Use Calendly link → calendly.com/david04calderon/ai-strategy-call

---

## PRE-CALL PREP (5 minutes before)

Run through this before every call:

1. **Google the company** — Find 1 recent news item, job posting, or LinkedIn post
2. **Check LinkedIn** — Look at the contact's title, tenure, past companies
3. **Review their form submission** — What did they say in their message?
4. **Check the lead score** (if from n8n automation) — What's the score and vertical?
5. **Pull up this framework** — Have it open during the call

**One sentence to open with:** Prepare a specific observation about their business. Example:
> *"I saw you guys just opened a second location in Kalamazoo — congrats. That kind of growth is usually when the back-office stuff starts to buckle."*

## THE CALL — MINUTE BY MINUTE

### 0:00–2:00 — OPEN

**Your lines:**
> "Thanks for taking the time, [name]. I want to make sure this is worth your 30 minutes — my goal today is to understand what's actually going on in the business and figure out if there's something specific we can help with. If it's not a fit, I'll tell you that directly and maybe point you to someone who is a better fit. Sound good?"

[They say yes.]

> "Great. Before I ask you anything — is there something specific that made you reach out today? Or a problem that's been sitting on your plate?"

**Then: SHUT UP.** Let them talk. Take notes.

---

### 2:00–15:00 — THE PAIN EXCAVATION

**Core questions — pick 4–5, don't use them all at once:**

**On the problem:**
- "Walk me through what that process looks like today, step by step."
- "Who's doing that right now — is it you, or do you have staff on it?"
- "How much time is that actually taking per week? Ballpark."
- "What happens when it goes wrong — what does a bad day look like?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

**On the cost:**
- "If I had to guess, what's the dollar cost of this problem? Revenue lost, hours wasted, whatever way makes sense to you."
- "What would it mean to your business if this was solved in the next 90 days?"
- "Is this the biggest problem right now, or is there something else keeping you up at night?"

**On urgency:**
- "Is there a forcing function here — a customer asking about AI, a competitor moving, a deadline?"
- "How long has this been a problem? Why now?"

**On authority:**
- "Is this a decision you'd make on your own, or does someone else need to be in the loop?"
- "If we found a solution that made financial sense — what does the approval process look like on your end?"

**Listen for:**
- Specific numbers (hours, cost, headcount)
- Emotional weight ("it's killing us," "I'm the one doing it at midnight")
- External pressure (customer mandate, competitor threat)
- Decision-making clarity (or lack of it)

---

### 15:00–22:00 — REFLECT AND EDUCATE (lightly)

**Reflect what you heard:**
> "Let me make sure I'm tracking this right. What I'm hearing is: [RESTATE THEIR PROBLEM IN THEIR WORDS]. That's costing you roughly [TIME/MONEY they mentioned]. And the urgency is [REASON THEY GAVE]. Is that accurate?"

[They confirm or correct.]

**Then — and only then — share what you've seen:**
> "Okay. I've seen this exact pattern with [automotive suppliers / medical practices / trucking companies — match their vertical]. What usually happens is [brief explanation of the root cause and why it compounds]. The way we typically solve it is [1–2 sentences on the approach — no jargon]."

**Key rule:** Give them a framework, not a pitch. You're showing pattern recognition, not selling a product.

**Example:**
> "The issue is almost never the work itself — it's that the process was designed for one truck and you're now running forty. The dispatch workflow never got rebuilt; it just got heavier. What we typically do is add an AI layer on top of your existing TMS so your dispatcher isn't the bottleneck anymore. Takes 3–4 weeks, doesn't disrupt what's already working."

---

### 22:00–27:00 — FIT CHECK

**Your lines:**
> "Based on what you've told me — I think there's a real fit here. Here's what I'd suggest as a next step: [OPTION A or OPTION B below]."

**OPTION A — Strong fit (score 7+):**
> "Let me put together a 1-page breakdown specific to your operation — what I'd build, rough timeline, and a number. I can have that to you in 48 hours. Then we can get on a second call for 20 minutes to review it. Does that work?"

**OPTION B — Possible fit (score 4–6, needs more info):**
> "I want to see your current process before I put a number on it. Is there a way to get a 30-minute walk-through of how [PROCESS] works today? I'd rather see the reality than guess. Can we schedule that?"

**OPTION C — Not a fit:**
> "I'm going to be straight with you — I don't think AI is the right answer for this right now, and I'd rather tell you that than waste your time and money. What I think you actually need is [REAL RECOMMENDATION — software, different consultant, hire someone]. Happy to point you to a few resources."

**Why be honest about no-fit?** Because that person will refer you to 3 people who ARE a fit. Your reputation in Michigan is a compounding asset.

---

### 27:00–30:00 — CLOSE

**Always end with a clear next step:**
> "So the next step is: I'll send you [what you promised] by [DATE]. You'll get an email from me within 24 hours. Any questions before we wrap up?"

**Before you hang up — ask this:**
> "Last question — is there anyone else at your company who should be in the loop on this? Or anyone in your network who has a similar problem? Even just a name to drop?"

**Why:** The construction/manufacturing Michigan community is tight. One happy conversation = multiple referrals.

## SCORING RUBRIC (use after every call)

Rate 1–5 on each dimension:

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pain clarity** | Vague problem | Specific process issue | Quantified dollar/time cost |
| **Budget signal** | No mention | Mentioned budget exists | Named a number or range |
| **Authority** | Not the decision maker | Influencer | Final decision maker |
| **Urgency** | "Maybe someday" | Active problem | External forcing function |
| **Fit** | Wrong vertical/size | Could work | Perfect ICP match |

**Score 20–25:** Proposal within 24 hours, priority treatment
**Score 14–19:** Proposal within 48 hours, standard follow-up
**Score 10–13:** Longer-cycle lead, one follow-up email, low priority
**Score <10:** Politely disqualify, refer elsewhere

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "We don't have budget for this right now."
> "I hear that. Two things: First, we can structure the engagement in phases so it's not one large check — we start with the highest-ROI piece and prove it before expanding. Second, depending on your employee count and business type, Michigan's Going PRO Talent Fund can cover a meaningful portion of the cost. It's worth understanding what you'd actually be paying before we call it a no. What's the number that would make this a yes?"

### "We already have software for that."
> "That's actually good to hear — most of what we build layers on top of existing tools, not instead of them. What are you using? [Let them tell you.] Okay — and is that solving the problem, or are you still doing the [SPECIFIC PAIN THEY MENTIONED] manually?"

### "I need to talk to my partner/CFO/board."
> "Totally makes sense — this is a business decision, not just a tech one. I'd rather have everyone in the room than have you advocate for something you're not 100% confident in. Can we schedule a 20-minute call with you and your partner? I'll bring the ROI breakdown and answer questions directly."

### "Can you give me a ballpark number?"
> "I can give you a range, but I'd rather give you an accurate number than a ballpark that turns out to be wrong. Based on what you've described — and I'm giving you a rough directional number — projects like this typically run $[LOW]–$[HIGH] depending on the complexity and integrations. But give me 48 hours and I'll give you a real number with a detailed scope. Is that fair?"

### "I've heard AI doesn't work / isn't ready."
> "A lot of the AI hype is garbage — I'll agree with you there. But the specific use cases we work on — [REPEAT THEIR PAIN POINT] — are boring, proven, and repeatable. This isn't ChatGPT making stuff up. It's structured automation that does one thing really well. I can show you case studies from companies your size if that would help."

### "Why should I use you vs. a big consulting firm?"
> "Honest answer: if you need a 200-page strategy deck and a 2-year engagement, a big firm is the right call. If you need something working in 4 weeks that you can show your customers and your P&L, that's what we do. Big firms outsource the build to junior staff. With us, I'm the one building it. You always know who's working on your account."
