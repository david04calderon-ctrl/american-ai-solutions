# 90-Day Revenue Model — American AI Solutions LLC
**Purpose:** Realistic cash flow projection for the first 90 days. Know what to expect, plan accordingly.
**Philosophy:** Under-promise to yourself, over-deliver in execution.

---

## THE HONEST BASELINE

**What month 1 of a new consulting business looks like:**
- Most leads take 2–6 weeks from first contact to signed contract
- Discovery calls don't close same-day
- Referral relationships take 30+ days to activate
- Cold email results don't show up until warmup is complete (14 days)

**What this means:** The work you do in Week 1 pays off in Week 5–8. Plant the seeds now.

## PRICING STRATEGY FOR MAXIMUM FIRST-90-DAYS REVENUE

**Don't discount your way to clients. But do offer a strategic entry point.**

### The AI Workflow Audit ($2,500, 48–72 hours)

The audit is not a loss leader — it's a real product. But it also has a 70%+ conversion rate to a larger Quick Deploy project. Think of it as a paid discovery call.

**Use it when:**
- The client is interested but hesitant to commit to a big project
- You're not sure if there's a fit (the audit surfaces the opportunity)
- You want to build trust before a larger engagement

**Upsell trigger:** At audit delivery, say:
> "Based on what I found, the highest-ROI automation for your operation is [X]. I can build this in 3–4 weeks. The audit cost applies to the project price. Want me to scope it?"

### The Monthly Retainer (Add to every proposal)

Starting at $1,200/month for ongoing support and optimization. This converts one-time clients into recurring revenue.

**By Month 6, target:**
- 3–4 retainer clients × $1,500/month average = $4,500–$6,000/month in predictable MRR
- This covers operating costs + personal expenses even in slow sales months

**How to pitch the retainer:**
> "Every system we build evolves. Payer rules change, new integrations become available, your operation changes. The retainer covers ongoing optimization, system health checks, and 2 hours/month of additional automation work. Most clients find they use it within the first week of every month."

## CASH FLOW MANAGEMENT

**Invoicing rules:**
- Always 50% upfront before work begins
- Never start work without the deposit
- Net 15 on final invoice (not Net 30 — you're a small business, cash flow matters)
- Accept: ACH, check, wire transfer, credit card via Stripe (but Stripe charges 2.9% — add to project price or absorb as a cost)

**Payment chase protocol:**
- Invoice sent → reminder on Day 8 if unpaid → phone call on Day 12 → final email on Day 15: "Work cannot continue until invoice is resolved."
- In the SOW: "Late payments accrue 1.5% interest per month after 15 days"
- Never let a client owe you more than 50% of the project at the end — you always have leverage with the final deliverable

**Quarterly estimated taxes (important):**
- Michigan: As a self-employed person in Michigan, pay federal + state estimated taxes quarterly
- Federal estimated tax deadlines 2026: April 15, June 16, September 15, January 15
- Rule of thumb: Set aside 30% of every payment for taxes in a separate savings account
- File as sole proprietor on Schedule C (if not electing S-Corp) or as LLC member
- **Talk to an accountant by Month 2** — QuickBooks Simple Start ($15/month) is enough to track income and expenses

## MINDSET FOR MONTH 1

Month 1 will feel slow. That's normal and correct.

The seeds planted in Week 1 (content, DMs, referral outreach, cold email warmup) compound. By Week 6, they start producing. By Week 10, the pipeline has enough velocity to feel like a real business.

The entrepreneurs who give up in Month 1 never see Month 4. The ones who plant relentlessly in the first 30 days are the ones writing the year-end income report.

**The three things that matter most in Month 1:**
1. Get in front of 75 new Michigan professionals per week on LinkedIn
2. Show up as a thought leader with carousel content
3. Build 3 referral relationships that will send warm leads

Everything else is secondary. Execution beats perfection in Month 1.
