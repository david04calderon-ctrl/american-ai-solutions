**Purpose:** Get on Google Maps and appear in "AI consulting Detroit" local searches. One-time setup, lasting SEO benefit.

**Impact:** Local search rankings, credibility, Google reviews pipeline

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS FIRST

When a Michigan business owner searches "AI consultant Detroit" or "AI automation Michigan" on Google, there are two types of results: organic web results and the local 3-pack (the map box with 3 business listings). Getting into the local 3-pack doesn't require outranking established companies on the web — it requires a well-optimized Google Business Profile.

With zero reviews, a complete profile still outperforms a competitor with no profile at all. And the reviews you collect over time compound indefinitely — each one makes the next easier to get.

## STEP 5: GET YOUR FIRST GOOGLE REVIEWS

Reviews are the single most powerful ranking factor for local search. You need 5 reviews to display a star rating. Target: **10 reviews in your first 60 days**.

### Your review link (get this after verification)

1. In your Google Business dashboard → Home → "Get more reviews"
2. Google gives you a short link like: `g.page/r/[your-business-id]/review`
3. Save this link — you'll send it to everyone

### Who to ask first (in this order):

**Round 1 (Week 1–2) — Warm contacts, no clients yet:**

These people can leave reviews based on your character, professionalism, and expertise — not project results. Perfectly acceptable on Google.

- **Family and close friends** who know you're starting this business
- **Former colleagues** who can speak to your technical skills and work ethic
- **Anyone who's attended a presentation** you've given
- **LinkedIn connections** you have a real relationship with

**Message to send:**
> "[Name] — I just launched American AI Solutions LLC and I'm building my Google presence. A quick review would mean a lot. Here's the direct link: [your review link]. Even a couple sentences is huge. Thanks in advance."

**Round 2 (Month 2+) — First clients:**

After every project delivery, ask directly:

> "[Name] — really glad the [project] is running well. One thing that would genuinely help me grow: a Google review. Here's the direct link, takes about 2 minutes: [review link]. Anything you'd be comfortable sharing about working together is great."

## STEP 6: POST ON YOUR GMB PROFILE (WEEKLY)

Google Business Profiles reward posting activity. Post once per week to stay active.

**4 types of posts:**

**1. Update post:** Share an insight or tip
> "Michigan Tier 2 and Tier 3 automotive suppliers: the Going PRO Talent Fund covers AI training costs up to $50,000 per year. Most don't know it qualifies — we structure every engagement to take advantage of it. [Link to blog post]"

**2. Offer post:** Promote your free strategy call
> "Free AI Strategy Call for Michigan Businesses — 30 minutes, written summary within 48 hours. Identify your highest-ROI automation opportunity. Book here: [Calendly link]"

**3. Event post:** When you speak at an event
> "Speaking at TechTown Detroit First Thursdays on July 3rd — 'AI for Michigan Business Owners: What's Actually Working in 2026.' [Event link]"

**4. Product post:** Highlight a specific service
> "AI Workflow Audit — $2,500, delivered in 48 hours. Map your highest-ROI automation opportunity before committing to a larger project. [Link]"

**When to post:** Tuesday or Wednesday morning — Google favors recency, and midweek beats weekends for business searches.

## MONITORING AND OPTIMIZATION

**Weekly (2 minutes):**
- Log into business.google.com
- Check: any new reviews? Respond to every review within 24 hours (even positive ones)
- Check: any questions submitted? Answer them

**Monthly:**
- Check your search query report: Home → Insights → "Searches used to find your business"
- Add any new relevant keywords to your description

**What a response to a positive review looks like:**
> "Thanks so much, [Name] — really glad the [project] made a difference for your operation. It's exactly why we focus on Michigan businesses. Appreciate you taking the time."

**What a response to a negative review looks like (rare, but be ready):**
> "Thank you for the feedback. I'd like to address this directly — please email me at david@americanaisolutionsllc.com so we can resolve this. Our goal is always for clients to feel the project exceeded their expectations."
