# Referral Partner Strategy — American AI Solutions LLC
**Principle:** In Michigan manufacturing and trades, one warm referral from the right person is worth 200 cold emails. Build this network systematically.
**Goal:** 5 active referral relationships generating 2–3 warm intros per month by Month 3

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS FOR MICHIGAN

The Michigan business community — especially manufacturing, trades, and logistics — is unusually tight. Business owners trust other business owners. The same people show up at TechTown events, Michigan Manufacturing Association meetings, and chamber breakfasts.

One referral from a trusted CPA or business banker to their manufacturing client is worth:
- 90%+ close rate vs. 5% cold outreach
- Zero acquisition cost
- Built-in credibility before the first call
- Faster sales cycle (days, not weeks)

This is the highest-ROI channel in the business. Build it before you need it.

---

## TIER 1 PARTNERS — HIGHEST PRIORITY

These people have daily access to your exact buyers. They're not competition. They have natural incentives to refer.

### 1. Business Bankers & Commercial Lenders

**Why they refer:** Banks want clients to grow and stay financially healthy. An AI efficiency play that improves margins makes loans safer. Bankers refer to demonstrate value beyond the account.

**Target contacts:**
- Commercial lenders at Flagstar, Chemical Bank (now TCF/Huntington), Mercantile Bank
- SBA loan officers at Michigan regional banks
- PNC Business Banking — Detroit metro
- Comerica — headquarters in Detroit, heavy Michigan manufacturing relationships

**Outreach approach:**
> "I work with Michigan manufacturers and contractors to automate workflows that are eating margin — quality inspection, dispatch, estimating. I'm building a referral network with commercial bankers because we often make clients better borrowers by improving their operational efficiency. Happy to reciprocate any referrals to clients who need SBA or equipment financing. Would you be open to a 15-minute coffee?"

**What to offer them:** Referral fee ($500–$1,000 per closed project) OR reciprocal referrals to their clients who need financing for equipment or expansion.

---

### 2. Business Accountants & CPAs

**Why they refer:** CPAs see every client's P&L. They're often the first to notice inefficiency ("you have 3 people in accounting doing reconciliation manually — that's fixable"). They want to look smart to clients by making introductions that save money.

**Target contacts:**
- Small and mid-size CPA firms in Metro Detroit, Grand Rapids, Lansing
- Plante Moran, Rehmann, UHY — Michigan-based regional firms with manufacturing clients
- Solo CPA practices serving construction and trades clients

**Outreach approach:**
> "I run an AI automation firm in Detroit focused on Michigan manufacturers and contractors. A lot of my work reduces the manual data entry your clients are paying their staff to do — which often shows up in your client's overhead line. I'm building referral relationships with local CPAs because you're usually the first to see where the operational inefficiency is. Would you be open to a quick call to see if there's a fit?"

**What to offer them:** Referral fee ($500 per closed project) OR a complimentary efficiency audit for one of their clients as a demonstration of your value.

---

### 3. Business Insurance Brokers

**Why they refer:** Insurance brokers with commercial clients talk to their clients every year at renewal. They want to add value beyond just the renewal conversation. AI automation that reduces workplace accidents (quality inspection, predictive maintenance) directly benefits the insurance relationship.

**Target contacts:**
- Michigan commercial insurance agencies (Acrisure, Hub International, local independents)
- Brokers specializing in manufacturing, construction, or fleet insurance

**Outreach approach:**
> "I work with Michigan manufacturers on AI-driven quality and safety systems. Better quality inspection and predictive maintenance directly reduce workers comp and liability claims — which makes your clients better risks at renewal. I'm looking for a few broker relationships where we can both deliver more value to shared clients."

---

### 4. IT Managed Service Providers (MSPs)

**Why they refer:** MSPs already have deep relationships with Michigan manufacturing and construction companies. They handle infrastructure but typically don't do AI or automation. This is a natural add-on referral.

**Target contacts:**
- Ntiva, LAN Systems, 2W Tech (Michigan MSPs with manufacturing clients)
- Any IT company that mentions "manufacturing IT" or "industrial networks" on their website

**Outreach approach:**
> "I run an AI automation firm in Detroit. My clients are mostly manufacturers and contractors — exactly the same base you're serving. I don't touch infrastructure, networking, or managed services, so there's no overlap. But when your clients ask you about AI, I'd love to be who you refer. Happy to return the favor when my clients need infrastructure work."

**What to offer them:** Referral fee ($750–$1,500 per closed project — these are higher-value deals) + white-label option for MSPs who want to offer AI as a service under their brand.

---

### 5. Business Attorneys & M&A Advisors

**Why they refer:** Business attorneys often work with companies going through growth, acquisition, or operational restructuring — exactly when AI automation becomes strategic. They want to add value to their relationship.

**Target contacts:**
- Michigan manufacturing attorneys (mergers, contracts, IP)
- Business transaction attorneys in Metro Detroit
- SBDC (Small Business Development Center) counselors — they advise dozens of businesses monthly

## HOW TO STRUCTURE THE REFERRAL RELATIONSHIP

### Option A — Referral Fee (simplest)
- $500 for projects under $15K
- $1,000 for projects $15K–$30K
- $1,500+ for projects over $30K, or ongoing retainer clients
- Paid within 30 days of client payment received
- Requires a written referral agreement (simple 1-page doc)

### Option B — Reciprocal Referrals (no cash, higher loyalty)
- You refer them to your clients who need their service
- They refer you to their clients
- Track informally — if it gets unbalanced, discuss
- No paperwork needed for first 90 days

### Option C — Co-marketing (highest brand value)
- Joint LinkedIn post: "I'm proud to partner with [their firm] to serve Michigan manufacturers"
- Featured in each other's email newsletters
- Joint webinar: "AI + Your Business Finances" (you + CPA partner)
- Co-host a breakfast event at a Detroit venue

**Best practice:** Start with a coffee meeting. Explain what you do, what your ideal referral looks like, and what you'd send to them. Ask for the same. No formal agreement needed in the first 90 days — just a handshake relationship.

## IDEAL REFERRAL PROFILE

Train your referral partners on exactly who to send you:

**Green light — refer immediately:**
- Michigan company (10–200 employees)
- Manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, or construction
- Currently has staff doing repetitive manual work (data entry, scheduling, reporting, inspection)
- Owner or senior ops person expressing frustration with inefficiency
- Any mention of AI from their customers or competitors

**Yellow light — ask more questions first:**
- Outside Michigan (we can work with them but may not qualify for Michigan grants)
- Very small (under 5 employees) — not enough operational complexity
- Pure retail, hospitality, or consumer service (not our core verticals)

**Red light — not a fit:**
- Pre-revenue startups
- Companies primarily looking for cheap software, not a build partner
- Anyone shopping purely on price

**The referral ask (give this to partners verbatim):**
> *"I work with a Detroit-based AI firm that helps Michigan manufacturers and contractors automate the manual work that's eating their margins. If you have a client who's growing but their back office hasn't kept up — operations, quality, dispatch, estimating — David at American AI Solutions is a great intro. First call is free and he'll tell you straight if there's a fit or not."*

## 90-DAY REFERRAL PARTNER BUILD PLAN

### Month 1 — Plant Seeds
- [ ] Identify 15 potential Tier 1 partners (5 bankers, 5 CPAs, 5 MSPs)
- [ ] Send cold outreach to all 15 (email + LinkedIn connection request)
- [ ] Book 5 coffee meetings
- [ ] Attend 1 TechTown or Chamber event

### Month 2 — Cultivate
- [ ] Follow up with Month 1 coffee meeting contacts (check in, share a useful resource)
- [ ] Identify 2–3 partners who "get it" and deepen those relationships
- [ ] Ask for first intro from 1–2 active partners
- [ ] Attend Michigan Manufacturing Association event

### Month 3 — Activate
- [ ] 5 active referral relationships in place
- [ ] 2–3 warm intros per month flowing
- [ ] First co-marketing post with strongest partner
- [ ] Formalize referral agreement with top 2 partners
