# Testimonial & Review Request System
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** Systematic approach to collecting, storing, and deploying social proof at every stage — from pre-first-client through mature operation.
**Why this matters:** One authentic testimonial does more for a $15,000 proposal than any amount of feature description. Prospects buying AI consulting are buying confidence in the person delivering it. Social proof provides that confidence.

---

## STAGE 1: PRE-CLIENT REVIEWS (Weeks 1–4)

Before you have paying clients, you can still collect reviews from people who know your work. This is legitimate — these people are reviewing your expertise, professionalism, and approach, not project deliverables.

### Who to ask (in order)

**Tier 1 — Closest relationships (ask immediately)**
- Former colleagues who can speak to your technical skills
- Former managers who can speak to your work quality
- Anyone you've helped with an AI question, even informally
- People you've presented or taught (webinar, Zoom, in-person)

**Tier 2 — Professional contacts (ask in Week 2–3)**
- LinkedIn connections you have a real relationship with
- Fellow entrepreneurs or business owners who've gotten to know you
- Event organizers or hosts who can speak to your expertise

**What they're reviewing:**
- Your knowledge and technical depth
- Your communication style (clear, not jargon-heavy)
- Your professionalism and reliability
- Their confidence in referring you

---

### Review request message — for warm contacts

**Google Review request (send the direct link):**

> "[Name] — I just launched American AI Solutions LLC and I'm building my Google presence. If you'd be willing to leave a quick review based on what you know of my work and how I operate, it would genuinely help me get started.
>
> The direct link: [g.page/r/YOUR-REVIEW-ID/review]
>
> Even two or three sentences is huge. Thanks in advance — and I'll return the favor whenever you need it."

**LinkedIn Recommendation request:**

> "[Name] — would you be willing to write a LinkedIn recommendation? Given your experience working with me on [specific project or context], your perspective would carry real weight for prospects considering working with me.
>
> Totally understand if now isn't the right time — just let me know. If yes, I'm happy to give you a few bullet points on what would be most helpful to highlight."

*(Note: Offering bullet points isn't ghostwriting — it's making it easy. Most people want to help but don't know what to say. Give them a focus.)*

---

### Bullet points to offer contacts for LinkedIn recs

> "If it's helpful, here are a few things I'd love for you to speak to — pick whatever feels authentic:
> - My technical depth on AI systems (how I explain complex things clearly)
> - How I approach problems (process-first, results-focused)
> - My reliability and follow-through
> - What you'd feel comfortable saying about referring me to someone in your network"

## STAGE 2: FIRST-CLIENT REVIEWS (After first project delivery)

This is the most important review you'll ever get. Handle it as a planned part of project delivery, not an afterthought.

### When to ask

**Ask at the 30-day check-in**, not immediately at delivery. Reasons:
1. At delivery, they're relieved the project is done — but they haven't seen the results yet
2. At 30 days, they've used the system and have real outcomes to report
3. A 30-day review reflects actual ROI, which is more convincing than a delivery-day review

**The one exception:** If a client expresses strong enthusiasm at delivery ("This is incredible — exactly what we needed"), ask right then. Strike when the iron is hot.

### 30-Day check-in email (results + review ask)

> Subject: 30-day check-in — [Project Name]
>
> [Name] —
>
> It's been about a month since we wrapped the [project name]. I wanted to check in on how it's performing.
>
> Quick questions:
> 1. How's the team adapting to the new workflow?
> 2. Are you seeing the time savings we projected? Anything higher or lower than expected?
> 3. Anything that needs a tweak or adjustment?
>
> If things are running well, I'd be grateful if you'd share your experience in a Google review. It takes about 2 minutes and has a real impact on how I'm able to grow. Here's the direct link: [review link]
>
> And if you have any colleagues who might be dealing with similar operational challenges, I'd be glad for a warm introduction — referral fees apply if they become a client.
>
> Talk soon,
> David

---

### In-person / on-call review ask script

At the 30-day check-in call, after discussing results:

> "I'm really glad to hear that [outcome]. One thing that would genuinely help me grow: if you'd be willing to share that in a Google review or a LinkedIn recommendation, it carries a lot of weight for prospects who don't know me yet. It takes about 2 minutes. Would you be willing to do that?"

*[If yes]:*
> "I'll text/email you the direct link right now so you don't have to search for it."

*(Send the link within 60 seconds. Strike while they're feeling good about the project.)*

## STAGE 3: TESTIMONIAL FORMATS FOR MARKETING USE

Once you have a review or recommendation, extract the most usable version for each channel.

### Format 1 — Full case study quote (for proposals and website)

**Best for:** Proposals, website testimonials section, email sequences

**Ideal quote structure:**
1. What the problem was before
2. What specifically changed
3. A concrete number if possible
4. A statement about working with David/AAIS

**Example of a strong testimonial:**
> "Before working with American AI Solutions, our quality inspectors were spending 3–4 hours per shift on paper logs. Now it's live in the dashboard and the OEM audit question that used to take us a day to answer takes 10 minutes. The grant documentation David handled also came back — we got $6,200 reimbursed. I'd recommend him to any Michigan manufacturer looking to get ahead of what the OEMs are going to ask for."
>
> — *[Name], Quality Manager, [Company Type], Michigan*

*(Note: If the client can't share their name/company due to competitive concerns, "Quality Manager, Tier 2 Automotive Supplier, Southeast Michigan" is still highly credible.)*

---

### Format 2 — Short pull quote (for social and collateral)

Extract 1–2 sentences that stand alone:

> "The OEM audit question that used to take us a day to answer now takes 10 minutes."
>
> — Quality Manager, Tier 2 Automotive Supplier

---

### Format 3 — LinkedIn post (you share it, tagged)

After getting a review or recommendation, write a post that references the result (not the review):

> "One of the things I hear most from Michigan Tier 2 suppliers after we deploy quality dashboards:
>
> 'The audit question that used to take a day now takes 10 minutes.'
>
> That's Level 3 Industry 4.0 maturity — real-time data visible to ops and OEM auditors without a binder hunt.
>
> 8–12 weeks from paper logs to dashboard. Michigan grants cover a significant chunk of the cost.
>
> If your shop is still on paper, 2026 is the year to change that."

*(This uses the result without requiring the client to be publicly named.)*

## VIDEO TESTIMONIALS (When You Have 3+ Clients)

A 60-second Loom from a client is worth 10x a written review.

**How to ask:**

> "[Name] — you mentioned on our last call that [result they shared]. Would you be willing to record a quick 60-second Loom — just you talking about what changed and what you'd say to a colleague in a similar situation? I'd use it on my website and in client conversations. Totally low pressure. If you're game, I can send you 3 bullet points to guide it."

**3 bullet points to give them:**
1. What was the problem before we started?
2. What specifically changed after the project?
3. Who would you recommend this to?

**Format for their recording:**
- Loom.com (free)
- No script needed — just candid
- Under 90 seconds
- Tell them: "Don't overthink it. The more authentic, the better."

## REVIEW RESPONSE SCRIPTS (For Google Reviews)

**Responding to a positive review:**

> "Thank you, [Name] — this means a lot. Helping [Company Name] move from [context] to [result] is exactly the kind of work we came here to do. Appreciate you taking the time to share this. — David"

**Responding to a critical review (rare, but be ready):**

> "Thank you for the feedback. I'd like to address this directly — please email me at david@americanaisolutionsllc.com. Our goal is always for every client to feel the project exceeded expectations, and I want to understand where we fell short. — David"

*(Never argue in the review response. Resolve privately.)*

| Timeframe | Target |

| Month 3 (first clients) | 10 Google reviews + 5 LinkedIn recs |
