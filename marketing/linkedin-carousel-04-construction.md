## SLIDE 2 — THE THREE BLEEDS

**Headline:** Where the margin goes before a nail is driven.

**The three biggest AI-solvable profit drains in Michigan construction:**

**1. Estimating Speed** — The average Michigan contractor takes 8–12 hours to produce a complete bid. Fast-moving GCs respond in 2–4. Every day of delay is a percentage of margin you give up just to win the bid. AI reduces this to 3–5 hours without compromising accuracy.

**2. Change Order Documentation** — The average commercial project has 15–35 change orders. Each one requires field photos, labor records, material receipts, and a formatted CO submission. A PM spending 45 minutes per CO on a 25-CO project burns 19 hours of billable management time. AI drafts it in 4 minutes from field notes.

**3. Project Reporting** — Owners, GCs, and subs all want weekly updates. Writing them is 2–3 hours/week per project manager that produces zero new value. AI generates them automatically from your PM software data.

These three alone represent 25–40% of a PM's workweek spent on paper, not projects.

## SLIDE 3 — THE ESTIMATING PROBLEM

**Headline:** Your estimator is your fastest asset. Right now, your process is making them slow.

**The current estimating workflow at most Michigan contractors:**
1. Receive plans (PDF, usually not organized by trade)
2. Do a manual takeoff (measure quantities by hand or in Bluebeam)
3. Look up material pricing (RSMeans, supplier quotes, memory)
4. Calculate labor hours (experience-based, spreadsheet)
5. Format the bid (Word or Excel template)
6. Review → adjust → send

**Total time: 8–12 hours for a standard commercial bid.**

**What AI changes:**
1. AI reads the PDF plans and extracts scope by trade automatically
2. AI pulls current Michigan material pricing from live supplier databases
3. AI applies your historical labor rates (from past project data)
4. AI generates a draft estimate in your format, flagging items it's uncertain about
5. Your estimator reviews and adjusts — 2–3 hours instead of 8–12

**Total time with AI: 3–4 hours.**

You can now bid 2–3x more jobs with the same estimator. At a 20% win rate, that's 2–3x more work booked.

## SLIDE 4 — CHANGE ORDER AUTOMATION

**Headline:** Every change order is money. Stop leaving it on the table.

**The change order reality:**
- 15–35 COs per commercial project (industry average)
- 30–60 minutes to document each CO correctly (photos, justification, labor, materials, format)
- Every CO that goes undocumented or under-documented becomes a dispute
- Michigan contractors leave an average of 8–12% of project value on the table through poor CO capture

**What AI does:**
1. Field crew submits a voice note or photo from the job site
2. AI converts it to a formatted change order with:
   - Description of the change (in the owner's required language)
   - Labor estimate (based on your rates and crew size)
   - Material list with current pricing
   - Supporting documentation attachments
3. PM reviews in 4 minutes → approves → submits to GC or owner

**Real result:** A 48-employee electrical contractor in Michigan went from 12% CO capture rate to 94% in 8 weeks. That's real money — recovered, not left behind.

## SLIDE 5 — PROJECT REPORTING AUTOMATION

**Headline:** Your PMs are spending 3 hours/week writing reports nobody reads the way they're written.

**The reporting burden:**
- Owner report: weekly project status, schedule, budget, open items
- GC daily log: progress, crew count, weather, issues
- Safety documentation: toolbox talk records, incident reports, JHA updates

**At a firm with 3 active PMs each managing 2–3 projects, that's 18–27 hours/week of report writing.**

**What AI automates:**
- Pulls schedule and budget data from Procore, Buildertrend, or Sage automatically
- Drafts the owner report in the owner's preferred format
- Generates daily logs from crew sign-in sheets and field photos
- Creates toolbox talk records and JHA documentation automatically
- Flags open items that haven't been addressed (AI watches for stalled RFIs, pending COs)

**The PM's job shifts:** From writing the report → reviewing the AI-generated report → adding context → sending. 25 minutes instead of 3 hours.
