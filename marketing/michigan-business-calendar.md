# American AI Solutions LLC

**Updated:** June 2026

---

## WHY TIMING MATTERS

Michigan business has strong seasonal rhythms. The manufacturer who is unreachable in December budget freeze will answer your call in February when the new fiscal year begins. The fleet operator who is slammed during Q4 peak season is relaxed and open to strategy conversations in January. The GC who can't talk during summer construction boom is ready to invest in efficiency tools in November.

Knowing these cycles means sending the right message at the right time to a prospect who is actually ready to hear it.

---

## JANUARY

**The January Mindset:** New budgets, new goals, fresh urgency to do things differently. The highest-receptivity month of the year for any strategic initiative.

**Manufacturing / Automotive:**
- New fiscal year budget releases — decision makers have authority to spend
- OEM quality planning kicks off for the year (Ford Q1 program, GM SQEP reviews)
- Annual supplier scorecards distributed — shops with poor data visibility just saw the number
- **Best outreach: "Start the year right" angle — data visibility before Q1 audits**

**Healthcare:**
- New year insurance changes → prior authorization surges
- Practice managers dealing with increased admin volume starting Week 1
- New CPT codes effective Jan 1 → documentation systems need updating
- **Best outreach: "Prior auth volume just went up — here's how to handle it without hiring"**

**Logistics:**
- Post-holiday slowdown = bandwidth for strategic conversations
- Annual carrier safety reviews and DOT compliance planning
- Fuel contracts being renewed — cost consciousness is high
- **Best outreach: Deadhead and dispatch efficiency ROI math**

**Construction:**
- Estimating season starts picking up — bid opportunities increasing
- Budget planning for equipment and technology
- Michigan construction show season begins (AGC events)
- **Best outreach: "Bid more, win more" — estimating AI for the season ahead**

**Events:**
- Michigan Manufacturers Association (MMA) Annual Planning Meeting
- AGC Michigan Winter Conference
- Detroit Regional Chamber New Year networking events
- TechTown Detroit — First Thursdays resume after holiday break

**Grant windows:**
- Going PRO applications open for Q1 (Michigan LEO processes quarterly)
- Industry 4.0 Tech Grant — new cycle starts January

**Construction:**

**Grant timing:**
- Q1 Going PRO applications: if applied in January, awards arriving now

## JUNE

**The June window:** Michigan fiscal year transitions. Many manufacturers' fiscal year ends June 30 (calendar or fiscal). Decision makers are looking to spend remaining budget or position for new budget.

**Manufacturing:**
- Fiscal year end (calendar or June 30) — remaining budget decisions
- "Use it or lose it" budget conversations
- OEM plants going into summer maintenance shutdowns → Tier 2/3 shops have downtime
- **Plant shutdowns are the BEST time to deploy AI — no production disruption**

**Healthcare:**
- BCBS Michigan mid-year review with practice groups
- Summer staffing challenges (vacations + volume)

**Logistics:**
- July 4 peak approaching — pre-holiday prep
- Summer fuel prices → cost consciousness for fleet operators

**Construction:**
- Ground-level activity at peak — long days, high revenue, no time for strategy
- Target outreach to owners (not foremen) — they're thinking about fall margins

**Content angle:**
- "Using the plant shutdown to upgrade your data systems" (manufacturing)
- "Summer logistics and the dispatcher who has to do it all" (logistics)

**Events:**
- TechTown First Thursdays — monthly through summer
- Detroit SBDC summer business development workshops

## JULY

**Summer mode:** Slower decision-making, but great month for relationship-building and thought leadership content. LinkedIn engagement actually rises in July (people are browsing more).

**Manufacturing:**
- Auto plants in summer shutdown (July 4 week + sometimes 2 weeks)
- Tier 2/3 shops have lighter schedules — **BEST month to book audit/scoping work**
- OEM supplier relations managers reviewing H1 performance

**Healthcare:**
- Summer scheduling complexity — coverage, vacations, locum docs
- Prior auth doesn't stop — actually spikes for elective procedures scheduled for fall

**Logistics:**
- Agricultural freight corridor at max intensity (western Michigan)
- Driver turnover peaks in summer — retention angle is most relevant

**Construction:**
- Highest revenue month for GCs — hard to reach but important to stay visible
- Target landscape, site prep, concrete subs who have lighter administrative burden than GCs

**Content angle:**
- LinkedIn carousel: "What the best Michigan manufacturers did during the July shutdown"
- Blog post: "How to use a slow week to map your highest-ROI automation opportunity"

**Construction:**

**Grant timing:**

## OEM AUDIT CYCLE (Michigan Automotive)

| OEM | Program | Typical Audit Timing | Michigan Impact |
|-----|---------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Ford | Q1 (Quality One) | Ongoing + annual reviews Q1/Q2 | Tier 2/3 suppliers feel pressure Jan–April |
| GM | SQEP (Supplier Quality Excellence Program) | Quarterly scorecards, annual audit | December–February scorecard distribution |
| Stellantis | SCORE (Supplier Cost Reduction Effort) | Q4 performance reviews → Q1 renewal | November–January is decision window |
| Honda | Supplier Scorecard | Semi-annual (spring + fall) | April and September/October |
| Toyota | TSSC (Toyota Supplier Support Center) | Annual (spring) | March–May |

**Key insight:** The weeks immediately following an OEM scorecard distribution are the highest-urgency period for Tier 2/3 suppliers. A bad data readiness score on a scorecard creates a buying trigger that no amount of cold outreach can replicate. Monitor LinkedIn for supplier posts about audit outcomes.

## MICHIGAN-SPECIFIC SEASONAL NOTES

**Auto shutdown weeks (avoid trying to reach plant-level contacts):**
- July 4th week (often extended to 2 weeks)
- Thanksgiving week
- Christmas/New Year's (Dec 23 – Jan 2)

**High-receptivity periods for cold outreach:**
- First 2 weeks of January (new year, new budget, fresh urgency)
- February (post-holiday, pre-spring, strategic planning window)
- September (pre-Q4, annual planning, audit prep)
- Early November (year-end urgency, last chance for Q4 projects)

**Michigan fiscal year patterns:**
- Most manufacturers: January–December (calendar year)
- Many healthcare practices: October–September (insurance year)
- Government-adjacent entities (schools, municipalities): July–June
- Construction: Generally January–December, but project-based

**Michigan weather reality:**
- January–March: Prospects are inside, answering phones, receptive to calls
- June–August: Shorter attention spans, more outdoor activity, harder to schedule meetings
- October–November: Perfect weather for in-person meetings if you want to do them
