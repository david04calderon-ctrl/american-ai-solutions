# Client Case Study Template
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** Capture proof of results from every completed project. Case studies are the most powerful sales tool you have — one real case study outperforms a month of cold outreach.
**When to create:** Within 2 weeks of project delivery. Don't wait.
**Privacy:** Always get client permission before publishing. Offer anonymous version if preferred.
**Distribution:** Website (Insights section), LinkedIn articles, proposal attachments, discovery call reference

---

## COLLECTING THE STORY (Do at 30-Day Check-In)

At the 30-day post-delivery call, ask these questions in this order:

**1. Baseline:**
> "When we started, what was the specific problem costing you the most time or money? Give me the number if you can — hours per week, cost per incident, whatever was measurable."

**2. Process:**
> "Walk me through what that looked like before. What was your team actually doing, step by step?"

**3. Result:**
> "Now that the system's been running for 30 days — what's the same number look like? Hours per week, cost, output — whatever changed most."

**4. Unexpected:**
> "What changed that you didn't expect? Anything that surprised you — positive or negative."

**5. Recommendation:**
> "If a peer of yours — same kind of business, similar size — asked you whether to do this, what would you tell them?"

Take notes. Don't record without permission. Use the exact words they use — don't paraphrase their results into your language.

[2–3 sentences. Paint the operational picture. What were they doing? What was it costing them? Who was affected?]

## The Approach

[2–3 sentences. What was built? How did it work? What did it connect to?]

We deployed a HIPAA-compliant prior authorization workflow built on the Anthropic Claude API under a Business Associate Agreement. The system reads incoming referrals, extracts diagnosis codes and patient data from [EHR system], builds the authorization request, and submits via payer portal API or fax. Exceptions — cases the AI flags as needing clinical judgment — are escalated to staff within minutes.

The build took 4 weeks. Staff training was completed in two 90-minute sessions. The training costs qualified for a $4,200 reimbursement through Michigan's Going PRO Talent Fund.

[Lead with the number. Then explain it. Then quote the client.]

**Prior auth processing time:** 22 hours/week → 4 hours/week

**Going PRO reimbursement received:** $4,200

The 18 hours recovered per week were reallocated to patient scheduling and follow-up — work the practice had been behind on.

"We didn't realize how much time we were losing until we got it back," said [Contact name]. "The staff who used to do prior auth now handle patient outreach we'd been putting off for months."

## WHEN YOU DON'T HAVE CASE STUDIES YET

Before your first project is delivered, use anonymized hypotheticals framed as projections:

> "For a 40-truck Michigan carrier at average mileage, cutting deadhead by 8 percentage points represents roughly $180,000 in annual recovered revenue and fuel savings. That's the kind of result our dispatch optimization model is designed to produce."

This is honest (it's a projection, not a claim) and specific enough to land.

The moment your first project delivers real results: replace every hypothetical with a real case study as fast as possible. Real numbers close deals. Projections open conversations.
