## THE MICHIGAN AI LANDSCAPE — GROUND TRUTH

### What's Actually Happening (Not What Headlines Say)

**The real adoption curve in Michigan:**
Most Michigan businesses are in the "awareness" stage — they know AI exists, they've seen the news, some executives have ChatGPT accounts. But almost none have deployed production AI in their operations. This is your window.

**The three categories of Michigan businesses right now:**
1. **Ignoring AI** (~55% of Michigan businesses) — will be disrupted or acquired in 5–7 years
2. **Experimenting with AI** (~35% of Michigan businesses) — using ChatGPT, Copilot, or off-the-shelf tools; seeing limited results because nobody's building real systems
3. **Deploying production AI** (~10% of Michigan businesses) — building actual automations, integrations, and custom models; capturing significant margin advantage

**Your clients come from group 2.** They've dipped a toe in, know it can work, but don't have the expertise to build the right thing. They're ready to pay.

## LOGISTICS / TRUCKING INTELLIGENCE

### Michigan Trucking Market Reality (2026)

**The market context:**
- Michigan has ~18,000 registered motor carriers (FMCSA data)
- Metro Detroit is a critical logistics hub — automotive supply chain + Great Lakes shipping + Canadian border crossings
- Owner-operators and small fleets (2–50 trucks) = 85% of Michigan carriers; these are your buyers
- Large carriers (Werner, J.B. Hunt, Swift) have their own AI teams — don't pitch them

**What's actually moving the needle for small/mid fleets:**
- Load board AI: DAT and Truckstop.com both have AI suggestions now, but they're basic. Custom load matching that factors in driver HOS, freight margins, and lane profitability is the upgrade.
- ELD data exploitation: Most fleets are collecting rich ELD data from Samsara or Motive but barely using it. AI that turns ELD data into actionable driver coaching and predictive maintenance flagging = immediate ROI.
- Driver retention modeling: Turnover prediction AI is the highest ROI play in trucking. Replacing a CDL driver costs $5,000–$12,000 in recruiting + lost productivity. The model is well-established.

**Key integrations for Michigan trucking:**
- ELD: Samsara, Motive (KeepTruckin), Verizon Connect, Geotab
- TMS: McLeod, TMW, Aljex, Tailwind, Axon
- Load boards: DAT, Truckstop.com
- Factoring: OTR Capital, Triumph Business Capital, RTS Financial (Michigan companies often factor)

**Regulatory context (always know this):**
- FMCSA Hours of Service rules: Any AI dispatch must respect 11-hour driving, 14-hour window, 30-minute break, 60/70-hour rules. This is a liability issue — always mention your system respects HOS.
- ELD mandate: All CMV operators with >16 hours are required to use ELDs. The data is already there — AI is the unlock.
- Drug & alcohol clearinghouse: FMCSA Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse — AI can automate the query compliance workflow

**Michigan-specific logistics grant angle:**
- MDOT (Michigan Department of Transportation) runs technology programs for freight efficiency
- Michigan Trucking Association: Industry group — good event networking
- US DOT's SMART Grants: Strengthening Mobility and Revolutionizing Transportation — federal technology grant for transportation innovation

## CULTURAL INTELLIGENCE — MICHIGAN BUSINESS NORMS

These matter in sales. Get them right.

**What Michigan business owners respond to:**
- **Directness**: Get to the point. Don't pad. "Here's the problem, here's the fix, here's the price" is preferred to a 20-minute preamble.
- **Show your work**: Michigan manufacturing culture values people who understand how things are made. Explain your technical approach without dumbing it down.
- **Local credibility**: Drop "Detroit" often. Michigan business owners prefer to keep money in Michigan. Use "Michigan business" not "small business" — they don't see themselves as small.
- **ROI language**: "This saves you X hours and recovers $Y" beats "this transforms your operations." Show the math.
- **Humility**: Don't oversell. If something hasn't been proven in production, say so. Michigan pragmatists respect honesty over sales polish.

**What to avoid:**
- Startup/Silicon Valley jargon: "disruptive," "unicorn," "move fast and break things" — earned eye-rolls in Michigan
- Vague timelines: "A few months" is unacceptable. "6 weeks, with these milestones" is credible.
- Over-promising: Better to under-promise and over-deliver than the inverse. Michigan B2B reputation is everything.

**Relationship timelines:**
- Construction and manufacturing: relationship-driven, slow to trust new vendors, but extremely loyal once trust is established. Expect 2–3 touchpoints before a yes.
- Healthcare: more compliance-focused, wants references and proof before committing.
- Trucking: fastest cycle — owner-operators are decisive. If it saves them money, they'll move in days.
