# Cold Calling Script
# American AI Solutions LLC

**Purpose:** Phone outreach for Michigan manufacturers, fleet operators, healthcare practices, and contractors. Use to supplement email sequences — call after Email 2 or 3 if no response.
**Target:** Operations managers, plant managers, practice managers, fleet managers, GC owners
**Goal of the call:** Get a 20-minute discovery call booked — not to sell on the phone
**Rule:** You are calling to qualify, not to pitch. Ask more than you talk.

---

## BEFORE YOU CALL

**Research first (2 minutes per prospect):**
- LinkedIn: What's their title, tenure, recent posts?
- Company website: What do they make/do, how big are they?
- Any news: expansion, new contracts, hiring surge, audit results?
- Do they know you already? (Connection, email open, LinkedIn interaction)

**Set a daily target:** 20 dials = 5–7 conversations = 1–2 qualified leads = 1 booked call every 3–5 days

**Best times to call:**
- Tuesday–Thursday, 7:30–9:00 AM and 4:00–5:30 PM
- Avoid Mondays (planning mode) and Fridays after noon
- Plant managers: catch them before the floor opens (7–8 AM)
- Practice managers: 12–1 PM (lunch coverage handoff window)
- Fleet managers: 6–8 AM (pre-dispatch window)

---

## GATEKEEPER SCRIPT

When you reach a receptionist or admin:

**Option A — Direct and confident:**
> "Hi, this is David Calderon calling for [Name] — is he/she available?"

*(Do not explain why. Gatekeepers route confident, direct calls differently than uncertain ones.)*

**Option B — If asked "What is this regarding?":**
> "I'm following up on some research I did on [Company Name]'s operations — specifically around [quality data / dispatch efficiency / prior authorizations / estimating]. I have a specific question for [Name] — is he/she in?"

**If they say they'll take a message:**
> "I appreciate that. I'll also follow up by email so he/she has context. What's the best email for him/her?"

*(This gets you the direct email AND names you in the gatekeeper's message.)*

## OPENING SCRIPTS — BY VERTICAL

### AUTOMOTIVE / MANUFACTURING

*[When prospect answers]*

> "Hi [Name], this is David Calderon from American AI Solutions — I'm based here in Detroit. I'll keep this brief — do you have 90 seconds?"

*[Wait for yes/okay]*

> "I work specifically with Michigan Tier 2 and Tier 3 suppliers on the AI readiness side — the quality tracking, OEE data, part-level traceability that Ford, GM, and Stellantis are starting to ask for in supplier audits. I'm calling because [Company Name] came up in my research and I wanted to ask — are you fielding any questions from your OEM customer about digital reporting or AI readiness right now?"

*[Let them answer — this is the pivot point.]*

**If YES:**
> "That's exactly the situation I work in. Can I ask — where are you currently in terms of quality data? Are your inspection logs digital or still paper-based?"

**If NO / not yet:**
> "You probably will be. GM's SQEP and Ford's Q1 process both added digital production monitoring requirements in the last 18 months. Most Tier 2 shops I talk to haven't gotten the formal ask yet, but it's coming. I'd rather help you get there before the audit than after. Is this something worth a 20-minute conversation?"

### HEALTHCARE

*[When prospect answers]*

> "Hi [Name], this is David Calderon from American AI Solutions LLC — I'm in Detroit. I know you're busy — do you have 60 seconds?"

*[Wait for yes]*

> "I build AI systems for Michigan healthcare practices — specifically around prior authorization, patient communication, and clinical documentation. The practices I work with typically get 18–20 hours per week of admin time back, under a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement. I'm calling because I wanted to ask — what does your prior authorization process look like right now? Is it a significant time drain on your staff?"

*[Let them answer]*

**If YES, it's a burden:**
> "That's consistent with what I see. 22 hours a week is the average I encounter. I've taken that to 4 hours with AI for three practices in Michigan. Would a 20-minute call be worth your time to understand how that works under HIPAA?"

**If NO / we have it handled:**
> "Good to hear. Can I ask what system you're using? [Listen.] I ask because some of the platforms I see practices using are HIPAA-compliant for storage but don't actually have BAAs for the AI processing component — which is a separate compliance requirement most people don't know about. It might be worth a 20-minute call just to confirm you're covered. Does that make sense?"

### LOGISTICS / TRUCKING

*[When prospect answers]*

> "Hi [Name], David Calderon — American AI Solutions, Detroit. Quick question if you have 60 seconds?"

*[Wait for yes]*

> "I work with Michigan fleet operators — typically 20 to 200 trucks — on dispatch and load optimization AI. I'm calling because I wanted to ask one question: how many trucks is each of your dispatchers handling right now?"

*[Let them answer]*

**If ratio is 30-40 trucks per dispatcher (typical):**
> "That's right in the range where the AI I build makes the biggest difference. We're getting operators to 65–80 trucks per dispatcher — same person, better tools, no overtime. The math on that is usually pretty compelling. Would 20 minutes be worth your time to see whether it pencils out for your fleet?"

**If they say they're doing fine:**
> "Fair enough. What are your biggest operational headaches right now? [Listen.] I ask because deadhead rate and driver retention come up constantly in my conversations with Michigan carriers, and the AI address both of those pretty directly. If either of those is on your radar, it might be worth a quick conversation."

### CONSTRUCTION / TRADES

*[When prospect answers]*

> "Hi [Name], this is David Calderon — American AI Solutions out of Detroit. Do you have 60 seconds?"

*[Wait for yes]*

> "I build AI estimating and project management systems for Michigan GCs and specialty trades. Quick question: how long does a typical bid take your estimating team right now?"

I've gotten that down to 3–4 hours for contractors in Michigan — using AI to handle the quantity takeoff and sub package assembly, so your estimator focuses on the judgment calls. And most of it qualifies for Michigan grant funding. Would 20 minutes be worth your time?"

**If they say they're fine or have estimating software:**
> "Good to know — what are you using? [Listen.] I ask because most of the software handles the database side but still leaves the takeoff and sub coordination as manual work. The AI layer I build sits on top of whatever you already have. But if your current process is tight, maybe the bigger opportunity is change order capture — that's usually where I find the most uncaptured margin with Michigan GCs. Is that something that's on your radar?"

## HANDLING COMMON RESPONSES

**"I'm not interested."**
> "Completely fair. Can I ask — is it that you're not looking at AI right now, or that the timing's just not right? [Listen.] Got it. Would it be okay if I sent you a one-pager on the Michigan grant funding that covers AI? Even if the timing's off now, knowing what's available is useful. What's your email?"

---

**"Send me an email."**
> "I'll do that right now — what's your email? [Get email.] And so I can make it relevant — what's the one process in your operation that costs the most time right now?"
*(This gets you a direct email AND qualifies the lead.)*

---

**"We already tried AI / we looked at it."**
> "I hear that a lot. Can I ask what you looked at? [Listen.] That makes sense — [validate their experience]. The gap I see in most failed AI pilots is that they started with a tool instead of a process. The system I build starts with a specific workflow that's costing you measurable time or money, and the AI targets exactly that. Would it be worth 20 minutes to see if that's a different approach than what you already tried?"

---

**"We don't have budget for that."**
> "That's actually why I'm calling — the Michigan Going PRO Talent Fund and Industry 4.0 grants cover a significant chunk of the cost. On a $15,000 project, most Michigan manufacturers are netting $6,000–$11,000 out of pocket after reimbursement. I know it sounds like I'm just saying that to get a meeting, but I handle the grant documentation myself — it's a real number. Would 20 minutes to run the math for your operation be worth it?"

---

**"We're too busy right now."**
> "I understand — that's usually when the AI makes the most sense, because the bottleneck is always a process, not people. When does it get less busy? [Listen.] Can I reach back out in [timeframe]? And in the meantime, I'll send you the grant overview so you know what's available when the timing is better."

---

**"Who are you with again?"**
> "David Calderon, American AI Solutions LLC — we're a Detroit-based AI consulting firm. I work specifically with Michigan businesses — manufacturers, healthcare practices, fleet operators, and contractors. We build systems that automate specific workflows, not strategy decks."

## THE ASK — BOOKING THE CALL

When the conversation is going well, make the ask clearly and directly:

> "Based on what you've told me, I think there's a real opportunity here. I do a free 20-minute strategy call — no pitch, just me walking through what I'd look at for your operation and telling you whether the ROI math works. If it doesn't work, I'll tell you on the call. Would [day] or [day] at [time] work?"

*(Give two specific options, not "when are you free." Specificity drives action.)*

- Note in HubSpot with the actual reason (not just "NI")
- Add to the 90-day follow-up sequence
- Record what they said about their current situation — it's intel

## CALL TRACKING (Log in HubSpot after every call)

**Required fields:**
- Outcome: Connected / VM left / Gatekeeper / No answer
- Disposition: Booked / Interested (follow up) / Not now (follow up later) / Hard no
- Notes: What they said about their current process, tools, pain points
- Next action: Follow-up date + action

**Weekly metrics to review:**
- Dials: target 100/week (20/day)
- Connect rate: 20–30% of dials
- Conversation to meeting rate: target 15–20%
- Meetings booked per week: target 3–5

Leave voicemails on the 1st and 3rd attempts only. Keep it under 20 seconds.

> "Hi [Name], David Calderon with American AI Solutions in Detroit — 313-[your number]. I work with Michigan [manufacturers/healthcare practices/fleet operators/contractors] on AI systems — specifically [quality dashboards/prior auth/dispatch optimization/estimating automation]. I had a quick question for you — call me back when you get a chance, or I'll try you again [day]. Again, David Calderon, 313-[number]. Thanks."

*(Do not leave a voicemail on the 2nd or 4th attempt — let it ring through. The pattern of voicemails creates familiarity before you connect live.)*
