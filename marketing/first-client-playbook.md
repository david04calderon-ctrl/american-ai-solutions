# First Client Playbook — American AI Solutions
## Land Client #1 with Zero Ad Spend | Start Today

---

## THE ONLY GOAL RIGHT NOW

One paying client. $2,500 minimum (the Audit tier).
That covers 2 months of operating costs and proves the model.
Everything after that compounds.

---

## WHERE CLIENT #1 COMES FROM

Not from ads. Not from the website. Not from cold calls.

**Your first client comes from someone who already knows you.**

Think through every person you've talked to in the last 2 years:
- Former coworkers
- Family members who own businesses
- Friends who own or manage businesses
- People you've met at events
- People in your LinkedIn network who run operations

One of them has a dispatch problem, a prior auth problem, an estimating problem, or an OEM compliance problem.

They just don't know you can fix it yet.

---

## DAY 1 — THE LIST (30 minutes)

Open your phone contacts. Open LinkedIn. Write down every person you know who:
- Owns a business
- Manages operations at a business
- Works in automotive, healthcare, logistics, or construction
- Has complained about a process being broken

Target: **20 names minimum.** Don't filter yet. Just list.

Then rank them:
- **Tier 1:** Decision makers. Can say yes to a $2,500 project.
- **Tier 2:** Influencers. Know someone who can say yes.
- **Tier 3:** Leads. Not ready yet but could refer.

Start with Tier 1.

---

## THE OUTREACH — What to Say

### LinkedIn DM (warm connection — they know you)

> Hey [Name] — hope you're good. I started something here in Detroit I'm excited about.
>
> We build AI automation systems for Michigan businesses — the stuff that's eating your team's time every day (dispatch, prior auth, estimating, OEM compliance). Fixed price, live in a few weeks.
>
> Are you dealing with any of that at [Company]? I'd love to run your operation by you and tell you honestly if there's something we can fix. 30 minutes, on me.
>
> — David

**Don't pitch. Ask.** The goal of Message 1 is to get a reply, not to sell.

---

### Text message (close contacts only)

> Hey [Name], quick question — are you still at [Company]? I'm building something that's directly relevant to what you do. Not selling anything, just want to run something by you. 10 minutes?

---

### LinkedIn DM (cold — you don't know them but you've seen them)

> [Name] — you run ops at [Company], right? I'm a Detroit-based AI firm that works specifically with [their industry]. I've been watching what OEMs/insurers/shippers are requiring from suppliers right now and I have some thoughts on how companies like yours can get ahead of it.
>
> Would a 20-minute call be worth your time? No pitch — just a real conversation about what we're seeing.

---

## THE FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE (if no reply)

**Day 3:**
> Wanted to follow up on my last message. No pressure if the timing isn't right — just want to make sure it didn't get buried.

**Day 7:**
> Last nudge on this. I'm working with a couple Michigan [industry] companies right now and one of the patterns I keep seeing is [specific pain]. If that sounds familiar at [Company], worth 20 minutes.

**Day 14 (final):**
> I'll stop following up after this — I know you're busy. If AI automation for your ops ever comes up, I'm the person to call in Detroit. Just wanted to plant that seed.

After that — move on. Respect their time. Revisit in 90 days.

## DETROIT BUSINESS COMMUNITY — Free Warm Leads

These are free, in-person or online, Michigan-specific:

**Facebook Groups (join today, contribute — don't spam):**
- Detroit Entrepreneurs
- Michigan Small Business Owners
- Detroit Business Network
- Michigan Manufacturing Network
- Michigan Healthcare Professionals

**What to post in these groups:**
> "Question for Michigan business owners — what's the one process in your operation that you wish ran automatically? I'm doing research for a project I'm building and want to understand where the real pain is."

You're not selling. You're asking. Replies become leads.

**Detroit events (show up):**

- Detroit Regional Chamber events
- MEDC (Michigan Economic Development Corp) events

One genuine conversation at one event can be worth more than 100 cold DMs.

## THE STRATEGY CALL SCRIPT — How to Close on the Call

When someone books the free 30-minute strategy call:

**Your only goal: book a paid engagement or give them a reason to refer you.**

**Open:**
"Thanks for making time. I did a little research on [Company] before the call. Tell me — what made you want to talk today?"

*Listen. Don't pitch for the first 10 minutes.*

**Diagnose:**
"Walk me through [the specific process they mentioned]. How many people touch it? How often does it run? What does it cost you when it goes wrong?"

**The honest moment:**
"Based on what you're describing, here's what I think we can do — and here's what I'd leave alone for now."

*This is where you earn trust. Be specific. Be honest. Don't oversell.*

**The close (if it's a fit):**
"I can put together a fixed-price proposal for exactly what we talked about. It'll be in your inbox within 48 hours. Does that work?"

**If they're not ready:**
"That makes sense. Here's what I'd do in your position before we revisit this. Mind if I send you some notes from this call? And if you know anyone else running [industry] ops in Michigan who's dealing with this — I'd love the intro."

## REFERRAL ACTIVATION — The Most Underused Free Channel

Every person you talk to knows 5 people you should talk to.

At the end of every conversation (strategy call, event, DM):

> "Who else do you know in Michigan running operations in [industry] that might be dealing with something like this? Even just a warm intro over text would mean a lot."

Most people are happy to refer when you've given them something valuable — even just a good conversation.

## THE REFERRAL FLYWHEEL — After Client #1

Client 1 pays → you deliver → you get a Google review + LinkedIn recommendation → you post the anonymized case study → 3 new leads see it → 1 becomes Client 2.

That's the entire growth model for Year 1.
Word of mouth in a tight market like Detroit moves fast.
Do the work right. Let the story travel.

---

## WHAT NOT TO DO

- Don't spend money on ads before you have 3 closed clients
- Don't cold call random numbers — it's legal but it burns goodwill
- Don't pitch on the first message — ask first, sell second
- Don't discount the rate — it signals you don't believe in the value
- Don't chase ghosts — if someone goes silent after 3 follow-ups, move on
- Don't wait for the website to be perfect before reaching out — it's good enough now
