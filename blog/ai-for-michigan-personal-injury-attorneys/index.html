Michigan PI Practice Areas: AI Applications by Case Type

Auto Accident — The Core Michigan Market

Michigan's roads generate consistent auto injury cases. I-94, I-75, US-23, and Michigan's rural two-lane highways all produce serious injury accidents. AI intake identifies which auto cases have threshold injury potential under Michigan's modified comparative negligence standard, whether the defendant has adequate coverage, and whether there are third-party liability angles (employer liability, road defect, product defect) that expand case value.

Premises Liability

Michigan slip-and-fall cases require proof of actual or constructive notice and often involve municipality immunity arguments. AI intake captures the specific premises information — location, condition description, incident time, owner identity, prior notice indicators — that allows fast preliminary viability assessment before committing attorney time to investigation.

Workers' Compensation Third-Party Claims

Michigan workers' comp cases with third-party liability components — a delivery driver injured in an accident, a warehouse worker injured by defective equipment — are often the highest-value personal injury cases in the state. AI screens for third-party liability indicators during workers' comp intake, flagging cases for PI attorney review that a standard comp intake process would miss.
