What AI Automates for Michigan Electrical Contractors

1. Instant Lead Response and Quote Intake

When a homeowner, contractor, or property manager contacts you about an electrical job, AI responds within 60 seconds. It asks the qualifying questions via text or web form: service type (panel upgrade, EV charger install, new construction rough-in, commercial tenant improvement), property type, timeline, and urgency. Armed with that data, AI routes emergency calls directly to your on-call crew and schedules estimate visits for standard work. Nothing sits in voicemail.

2. Estimate Follow-Up Sequences

You send a quote. The customer goes silent. Most electrical contractors follow up once and move on. AI runs a 5-touch follow-up sequence: day 1 confirmation, day 3 value prompt ("Our team is licensed master electricians — here's what that means for your permit"), day 7 availability update, day 14 re-engagement, day 21 final offer. Estimate close rates improve 35–45% with systematic follow-up vs. one-and-done.

3. Crew Scheduling and Permit Coordination

AI manages your production calendar, clustering jobs by geography and permit jurisdiction (each Michigan municipality has different permit timelines and inspection scheduling systems). When a job is confirmed, AI sends the crew assignment, permit application reminder, and inspection scheduling prompt to your office — all timed to the job's timeline. Nothing falls between the cracks on a multi-day commercial job.

4. Project Status Updates to Customers

Customers want to know when their electrician is arriving, when the permit is approved, and when the inspection is scheduled. AI sends automated project updates at each milestone — no more "Is your crew coming today?" calls that interrupt your field team. Customers who feel informed are customers who leave 5-star reviews.

5. Review Requests and Referral Campaigns

Three days after job completion, AI sends a Google review request. Thirty days later: a referral ask to neighbors and friends. For commercial clients: a re-engagement campaign 90 days out ("Time for your next scheduled electrical inspection or upgrade?"). Your past clients become your best lead source — automatically.

Michigan Electrical Market Intelligence

Residential: Panel Upgrades and EV Readiness

Michigan's housing stock — much of it pre-1980 with 100-amp service panels — needs panel upgrades to support EV chargers, whole-home generators (increasingly popular in Michigan's storm-prone climate), and modern appliance loads. AI intake workflows that pre-qualify panel upgrade and EV charger jobs (current panel amperage, service entrance type, garage location, desired charger level) generate better-qualified leads for estimate visits.

Commercial: Healthcare and Manufacturing

Michigan's healthcare facility expansion (Corewell Health, Henry Ford, Beaumont, Sparrow) and manufacturing plant upgrades drive significant commercial electrical work. These jobs require bid proposals, certified payroll documentation (if prevailing wage), and ongoing permit coordination. AI generates commercial bid summaries from your template and tracks permit milestones for multi-month commercial projects.

Michigan LARA Licensing and Permit Compliance

Michigan requires electrical permits pulled by licensed master electricians (Michigan Electrical Administrative Act, PA 217). AI workflows that automatically confirm licensing status, generate permit application checklists for each Michigan municipality, and track inspection scheduling ensure that administrative compliance doesn't slow down your crews.
