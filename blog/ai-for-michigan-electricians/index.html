Michigan Electrical Contractors Win on Quality — and Lose on Follow-Up

A Michigan electrician who sends 20 estimates per month and converts 50% is leaving $48,000 per month on the table from the 10 estimates that go cold. Most electrical contractors do not have a systematic follow-up process. They send the estimate and hope. AI follows up every estimate at Day 2, Day 5, and Day 10 with messages tailored to the specific project — converting cold estimates into booked jobs at rates 30 to 40% higher than manual follow-up.

A Michigan electrical contractor who improves estimate conversion from 50% to 65% on 20 monthly estimates adds 3 additional jobs per month. At $4,800 average per residential job, that is $172,800 in additional annual revenue from the same marketing spend.

Estimate Follow-Up Sequences

After sending every estimate, AI fires a follow-up sequence: Day 2 "Just wanted to make sure you received the estimate for [project]. Happy to answer any questions or adjust scope if needed." Day 5 "Your project timeline is still workable — we have availability starting [date]. Did the estimate look right for your budget?" Day 10 "Last check-in on the estimate. We have a slot opening up next week that would work well for your project. Want to move forward?" Estimate follow-up sequences recover 25 to 35% of stalled estimates that would otherwise go to a competitor.

Service Agreement and Maintenance Programs

Commercial and property management clients need recurring electrical inspections, panel maintenance, and code compliance checks. AI outreaches past commercial clients with a maintenance agreement offer: "We serviced your property at [address] in [month]. Michigan electrical code requires annual inspections on commercial panels. Want to set up a maintenance agreement to keep you compliant and avoid emergency rates?" Maintenance agreements convert at 30 to 45% for past commercial clients and create predictable monthly revenue during slow seasons.

Emergency Response Lead Capture

Emergency electrical calls are the highest-margin work in the trade. AI captures every after-hours inquiry and responds immediately: "We received your emergency request. Our licensed electrician is available within [timeframe]. Confirming your address and the issue so we can dispatch." Immediate response on emergency requests captures 70%+ of callers who would otherwise call the next electrician on their list.

Past Customer Reactivation

Michigan homeowners who had electrical work done 2 to 3 years ago are prime candidates for panel upgrades, EV charger installations, generator additions, and code update work. AI sends a past customer sequence: "It has been [X years] since we completed the [project] at your home. EV charger installations and whole-home generator systems are the most popular upgrades we are doing right now. Want a free assessment?" Past customer reactivation converts at 25 to 40% because they already trust the work.

Referral and Review Campaigns

Electrical referrals are worth $4,800 to $18,500 each. AI sends a referral request after every completed job: "We really appreciate the opportunity to work on your [project]. If you know a neighbor, landlord, or business owner who needs electrical work, we would love the introduction. We treat every referral like family." Referral campaigns generate 30 to 45% of new work for established electrical contractors.

EV Charger and Generator Upsell Campaigns

Michigan EV adoption is growing 40% year-over-year. AI sends a targeted campaign to all residential customers who had electrical work in the last 5 years: "Michigan EV charger installations typically run $800 to $1,500 installed and may qualify for a federal tax credit. Are you planning to go electric? We are booking charger installations 3 weeks out right now." EV charger campaigns convert at 20 to 30% with the tax credit angle.
