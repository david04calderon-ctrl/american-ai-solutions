Where Michigan Vet Practices Leave Revenue on the Table

1. Vaccine and Wellness Recall Campaigns That Don't Get Sent

Most practices have hundreds of patients overdue for annual wellness exams, heartworm tests, or booster vaccines. The data is in your practice management software. The problem is someone has to pull the list, draft the messages, and send them — and that someone has fifteen other things to do. AI pulls the list automatically and sends personalized recall reminders on schedule. Recall compliance typically improves 25–40%.

2. No-Shows and Appointment Gaps

A missed appointment in a busy vet practice isn't just lost revenue — it's a slot that another sick pet couldn't get. AI sends appointment reminders via text 48 hours and 24 hours before each visit, with a confirm/reschedule link. No-show rates drop 35–50%.

3. New Clients Who Don't Return

A new puppy owner brings their dog in for the first wellness visit. They have a great experience. And then... nothing. They go somewhere convenient when the next need arises. AI sends a follow-up 3 days after the first visit, then a recall at the 1-year wellness mark, and a personalized birthday message for their pet. First-time-to-loyal conversion improves significantly.

What the AI System Runs Automatically

  • Appointment reminders: 48-hour and 24-hour text reminders with confirm/reschedule links
  • Vaccine recall campaigns: Auto-triggered when a patient is 30/60/90 days overdue for their next vaccine
  • Annual wellness recall: Sent 11 months after last annual exam — "time for [pet name]'s annual checkup"
  • Post-visit follow-up: "How is [pet name] feeling?" 48 hours after any sick visit — builds loyalty, catches complications
  • Heartworm / flea season reminders: Michigan-timed for May and September
  • Pet birthday messages: Personalized annual "Happy Birthday!" with a wellness reminder attached
  • Review requests: Sent to satisfied clients after positive appointments
  • Lapsed client re-engagement: Clients not seen in 18+ months get a personalized "checking in on [pet name]" message
Michigan Angle: Michigan's tick and heartworm season runs May through October. Seasonal prevention reminders — timed to Michigan's actual climate, not a national average calendar — convert at significantly higher rates than generic reminders. AI sends these at the right time, every year, automatically.
