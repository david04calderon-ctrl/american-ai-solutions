Physical therapy is unique among healthcare specialties: outcomes depend on consistency. A patient who attends every session in their care plan gets better. A patient who drops off at session 6 of 12 doesn't — and often ends up back in pain within months. That early dropout costs your practice revenue and costs your patient their recovery.

AI keeps patients on track. Automatically.

Where Michigan PT Practices Lose Revenue

1. No-Shows and Cancellations Without Rebooking

PT no-shows are more expensive than most healthcare specialties because sessions are longer and slots are harder to fill last-minute. AI sends appointment reminders 48 hours and 24 hours before each session with a confirm link — reducing no-shows 35–50%. When someone does cancel, AI immediately offers a same-week rebook slot.

2. Care Plan Dropouts

Studies show 40–70% of PT patients don't complete their prescribed care plan. Most of those dropouts happen between sessions 4–8. AI monitors each patient's attendance pattern and sends a personalized check-in when someone misses a session or goes more than 10 days without booking — catching the dropout before it becomes a permanent gap.

3. Referral Source Opportunities Not Captured

Michigan PT practices get most of their patients from physician referrals. AI helps you systematically follow up with referring physicians — quarterly check-ins, outcome summaries for shared patients, and referral relationship maintenance — without adding admin burden.

Automation Workflows Built for PT Practices

  • Appointment reminders: 48-hour and 24-hour text reminders with confirm/reschedule links
  • Dropout detection: Flags patients who miss a session and triggers a personal outreach within 24 hours
  • Care plan progress messages: "You're halfway through your plan — great work!" — motivational check-ins that improve completion rates
  • Discharge follow-up: 30-day post-discharge check-in to assess outcomes and open the door to maintenance care
  • Referring physician outreach: Quarterly automated updates for each physician in your referral network
  • Review requests: Sent to patients at discharge when satisfaction is high
Michigan Angle: Michigan's industrial workforce — automotive, construction, manufacturing — has among the highest rates of musculoskeletal injury in the country. PT practices that serve this demographic deal with workers comp cases, return-to-work timelines, and union health plans.
