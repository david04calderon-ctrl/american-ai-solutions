What AI Automates for Michigan Physical Therapy Clinics

1. Digital Patient Intake and Insurance Verification

New patients complete intake forms digitally before their first appointment — demographics, medical history, functional goals, physician referral information. AI simultaneously verifies insurance eligibility, checks PT benefit limits (visits per year, copay, deductible status), and flags any prior authorization requirements. You walk into the eval knowing the patient's insurance situation completely, before you've said hello.

2. SOAP Note Generation

After each patient session, the therapist dictates a brief voice summary or completes a structured prompt: what was worked on, patient response, functional progress, plan for next session. AI generates a complete, standards-compliant SOAP note formatted for your EMR (WebPT, Clinicient, or Therabill). The note is ready for review and e-signature in under 5 minutes. What used to take 15–20 minutes per patient happens in the time it takes to walk to the next treatment room.

3. Prior Authorization Workflow

Michigan insurers requiring prior auth for PT — particularly for orthopedic and neurological cases — demand specific clinical justification documentation. AI generates prior authorization requests from the patient's intake data, physician referral, and initial evaluation findings. The clinical summary is formatted to each payer's submission requirements (BCBSM, Priority Health, HAP, Aetna, United). Staff submit; AI did the writing.

4. Home Exercise Program Generation

HEP documentation is a time sink — therapists writing out exercises by hand or clicking through HEP builder databases for every patient. AI generates customized home exercise programs based on the patient's diagnosis, treatment phase, and functional limitations. The HEP is formatted for patient comprehension and ready to print or send digitally. Patients get better HEPs. Therapists spend 10 minutes less per patient doing clerical work.

5. Recall and Schedule Management

Patients who complete a PT episode are prime candidates for future episodes — post-surgical, injury recurrence, or maintenance care. AI sends a 90-day post-discharge check-in, a 6-month wellness outreach, and a re-referral prompt. Physician referral relationships are maintained with automated outcome summary reports sent to referring physicians after discharge. Your referral network feeds itself.

Michigan BCBSM Intelligence: Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan — the state's dominant commercial insurer — has specific PT authorization criteria for spinal conditions, post-surgical rehabilitation, and chronic pain management. AI prior auth workflows pre-loaded with BCBSM's clinical criteria documentation requirements reduce denial rates and reconsideration work by 40–60% compared to manual submission.
