Michigan Mortgage Compliance: What AI Must Get Right

Michigan Mortgage Compliance Requirements for AI Systems

RESPA (12 CFR Part 1024): AI communications with borrowers must not constitute settlement service kickbacks or unearned fees. Automated referral partner communications must be reviewed for RESPA compliance before deployment.

Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) / Fair Housing Act: AI lead scoring and routing systems must be regularly audited for disparate impact. Michigan's Elliott-Larsen Civil Rights Act adds state-level protected class requirements. AI systems that influence which leads receive faster response times require bias testing documentation.

TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure): AI-generated communications must not contain fee estimates that could be construed as the Loan Estimate. Any AI communication discussing costs must include appropriate disclaimers.

NMLS / Michigan DIFS: AI cannot provide loan advice, rate quotes, or APR representations without an MLO license holder reviewing and approving. AI pre-qualification conversations must end with a handoff to a licensed LO before any rate or program commitments are made.

Data Security: NPI (Non-Public Personal Information) under Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act requires documented security programs. All AI systems handling borrower financial data must meet GLB Safeguards Rule requirements (16 CFR Part 314).

Michigan-Specific Mortgage Market Intelligence

Michigan's mortgage market has dynamics that national platforms don't account for — and that AI can be trained to recognize:

  • MSHDA Programs: Michigan State Housing Development Authority programs (MI Home Loan, MI Home Loan Flex, Down Payment Assistance) have specific eligibility requirements, income limits, and purchase price caps that vary by county. AI can pre-screen borrowers for MSHDA eligibility and route MSHDA-eligible leads to LOs with MSHDA delegated underwriting approval.
  • Michigan Land Contract Market: Michigan has a significant land contract market, especially in rural areas. AI workflows can identify land contract buyers who may be ready to convert to conventional financing and initiate appropriate outreach sequences.
  • Detroit Neighborhood Programs: Detroit has specific mortgage programs through Detroit Home Mortgage, Detroit Land Bank Authority, and Wayne County ARRA funds. AI can flag Detroit-city properties and ensure appropriate program screening.
  • Seasonal Purchase Volume: Michigan's purchase market is intensely seasonal — spring is compressed and competitive. AI can help LOs prepare referral partner communications, rate alerts, and pipeline acceleration tools ahead of the March–June spike.
