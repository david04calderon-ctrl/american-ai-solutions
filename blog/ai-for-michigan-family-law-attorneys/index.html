Where AI Delivers the Highest ROI in Family Law

1. Automated Client Intake & Conflict Checks

Before an attorney spends a minute on a potential client, AI screens the intake form: checking for conflicts, flagging incomplete financial information, and scoring urgency (emergency custody situations vs. uncontested divorce). Only qualified, cleared prospects reach the attorney's desk — fully briefed and pre-analyzed.

2. Document Drafting at Scale

Divorce settlement agreements, parenting time plans, child support worksheets, and QDRO language all follow predictable templates with variable inputs. AI drafts these documents from structured case data — what used to take a paralegal 3 hours takes 12 minutes. Attorneys review and sign, not type and format.

3. Discovery Organization & Timeline Building

Financial disclosure packets, bank statements, tax returns, and asset inventories arrive in disorganized formats. AI extracts key figures, identifies inconsistencies, and builds clean timelines of marital assets and income — the kind of analysis that previously required expensive forensic accountants for straightforward cases.

4. Client Communication Automation

Family law clients are anxious and call constantly. AI handles routine status updates ("your hearing is scheduled for July 14 at 9am in Courtroom 3"), answers FAQ-level questions, and escalates only genuine legal questions to the attorney. Clients feel heard; attorneys aren't interrupted 20 times a day.

5. Court Date & Deadline Tracking

Missing a motion deadline or a mandatory disclosure date is a malpractice risk in family law. AI monitors every active matter, sends 14-day and 48-hour reminders to both attorneys and clients, and flags conflicts when hearings stack on the same day.

Michigan Specific: Michigan's Friend of the Court system generates mandatory FOC forms, recommendations, and objection deadlines that vary by county. AI trained on Oakland, Wayne, Macomb, and Kent County FOC procedures can auto-populate county-specific forms and track objection windows — a real differentiator for multi-county practices.
