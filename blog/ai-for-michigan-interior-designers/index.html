What AI Does for Michigan Interior Design Firms

Client Intake and Discovery Automation

A potential client reaches out. Your AI system sends a comprehensive intake questionnaire — style preferences with visual examples, budget parameters, project scope, timeline, contractor relationships, and pet or family lifestyle factors. Responses feed into a structured project brief that's ready for your first consultation call. You arrive prepared with a pre-built project profile instead of starting from scratch. Consultation conversion rates climb because clients feel understood before they walk in the door.

Proposal Generation

AI generates your project proposals from the discovery intake — outlining scope, phasing, design fee structure, procurement responsibilities, and timeline — in your firm's voice and format. A proposal that used to take 6 hours takes 90 minutes: 30 to review the AI draft and 60 to add the visual elements and personal touches that close the project. You send more proposals. You win more projects.

Vendor Sourcing Research

Sourcing is where interior designers lose the most time — searching trade websites, cross-referencing lead times, checking availability in the right dimensions and finishes, comparing prices across vendors, and verifying that pieces work together aesthetically and functionally. AI performs this research across your preferred vendor network in minutes: you specify the requirements (dimensions, style direction, budget range, lead time constraint), and AI returns a curated shortlist with comparison data. You make the selection. AI handles the order documentation and follow-up.

Project Management and Contractor Coordination

AI tracks your active projects against timeline milestones, sends automated update emails to clients on a schedule you control, and manages contractor coordination touchpoints — delivery confirmations, installation scheduling, punch list documentation. You stop being the bottleneck in every project's communication chain. Clients feel informed without you spending 2 hours per day on emails.

Michigan Design Markets: Residential and Commercial

Oakland County Residential Premium Market

Bloomfield Hills, Birmingham, and Franklin are Michigan's highest-value residential interior design markets. Full-home renovations in these markets run $500K–$2M+ in furnishing and design budgets. Clients expect professional, prompt communication and meticulous project documentation. AI systems that generate daily project updates, track procurement milestones, and communicate proactively when lead times shift differentiate your firm in a market where client expectations are very high.

Northern Michigan Lake Home Design

Michigan's lake home market — Traverse City, Petoskey, Harbor Springs, Charlevoix, and the UP's Keweenaw Peninsula — generates significant design work from out-of-state homeowners who need remote-capable, highly organized design teams. AI enables virtual-first client workflows: digital intake, presentation software, remote sourcing presentations, and clear communication through every project phase without requiring clients to be present for every decision.

West Michigan Commercial and Hospitality

Grand Rapids' hotel, restaurant, and office design market benefits from deep furniture industry connections — Herman Miller, Steelcase, and Haworth are all headquartered within an hour. AI sourcing tools that integrate with these manufacturers' trade programs give West Michigan designers speed advantages on commercial projects that rely heavily on contract furnishings.
