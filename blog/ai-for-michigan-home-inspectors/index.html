What AI Automates for Michigan Home Inspectors

1. Instant Scheduling and Confirmation

When an agent or buyer calls or texts to schedule an inspection, AI responds within 60 seconds with available time slots, confirms the appointment, and sends calendar invitations to the buyer, agent, and you. Property address is captured, appointment details are logged, and a pre-inspection information packet goes to the buyer automatically. Agents never wait on hold. You never play phone tag between inspections.

2. Report Writing Assistance

After completing an inspection, AI assists with report generation — formatting field notes from your inspection app (HomeGauge, Spectora, or Horizon) into properly structured report sections with consistent language. It catches missing fields, suggests standard language for common Michigan issues (knob-and-tube wiring in older Detroit housing stock, basement water intrusion in spring thaw areas, furnace age in Michigan's cold-climate homes), and produces a professional draft for your final review. What took 2–3 hours takes 45 minutes.

3. Agent Relationship Campaigns

AI runs a monthly agent stay-in-touch campaign — market updates, inspection tip articles, Michigan real estate content — to every agent in your referral network. Agents who hear from you regularly refer you consistently. Agents who don't hear from you refer whoever they thought of last. AI makes you the one they think of.

4. Client Post-Inspection Follow-Up

After report delivery, AI sends a 30-day follow-up to the buyer: "Have you had any questions about items in your report since moving in?" This single touchpoint generates Google reviews, referrals to friends buying homes, and repeat business when clients buy again. Most inspectors never follow up. You do — automatically.

5. Ancillary Service Upsells

Radon testing, sewer scope, mold inspection, thermal imaging, pool inspection, and WDO inspection are all upsell opportunities that most inspectors mention verbally and most clients forget. AI sends a pre-inspection upsell prompt to every client: "We offer radon testing for $75 — highly recommended for Michigan basements. Reply YES to add it." Ancillary revenue increases 20–30% with systematic upsell prompts vs. verbal-only.

Michigan Housing Stock Intelligence: Michigan has an older housing stock than most states — Detroit and its suburbs contain significant pre-1960 construction with specific inspection focus areas: lead paint (pre-1978), knob-and-tube wiring, galvanized supply lines, older HVAC systems, and Michigan-specific basement water intrusion from spring thaw. AI report templates built around Michigan's housing stock produce more thorough, consistent reports than national templates.

Michigan Home Inspection Market Intelligence

Southeast Michigan: Volume and Speed

Metro Detroit's high-volume purchase market — Oakland, Macomb, Wayne, and Washtenaw counties — demands scheduling turnaround measured in hours. Agents working competitive offers need inspections within 3–5 days of acceptance. Inspectors with instant scheduling confirmation capture more bookings from time-pressured agents than those who call back when they get a chance.

Northern Michigan: Seasonal Second Homes

The northern Michigan second-home market — Traverse City, Petoskey, Charlevoix, Leelanau Peninsula — involves buyers who are often out of state and rely heavily on inspector professionalism and clear communication. Inspectors serving this market need polished digital communication, virtual walkthrough capabilities, and AI-assisted report delivery systems that work for buyers reviewing reports from Chicago or Detroit.

Michigan InterNACHI and ASHI Compliance

Michigan home inspectors operating under InterNACHI or ASHI standards need consistent documentation. AI report workflows built to Michigan-specific templates (with appropriate scope-of-work language, limitation statements, and disclosure requirements) ensure compliance with Michigan's Home Inspector Registration Program (BIRR) requirements.
