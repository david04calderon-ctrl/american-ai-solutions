Six AI Automations Built for Michigan Funeral Service

Death Certificate Preparation

AI pre-populates Michigan death certificate fields from the intake form data, pulling decedent demographics, cause of death information from medical records (when provided), and disposition details. Reduces preparation time from 2.5 hours to under 45 minutes. Funeral director reviews and certifies — AI handles the data entry.

Obituary Drafting

AI generates a complete obituary draft from the family intake form — incorporating biographical information, survivors, service details, and family-provided memories. Draft presented to the family for review and personalization. Eliminates the blank-page problem and reduces average obituary time from 90 minutes to 20 minutes.

Aftercare & Grief Support Communication

Automated, personalized grief support sequence for every family: a 1-week check-in card, 30-day condolence letter, 6-month anniversary acknowledgment, and 1-year remembrance — all in your funeral home's voice and brand. Builds lasting community relationships without adding staff workload.

Preneed Account Administration

AI tracks preneed contract anniversary dates, required trust statement distributions, and Michigan preneed statutory compliance deadlines — alerting your preneed director 30 days before each deadline. Generates trust accounting summaries for annual statutory filings. Reduces preneed administrative time by 60%.

Vendor & Permit Coordination

AI manages the supplier communication for each case — casket/urn orders, cemetery and crematory permits, flower vendor coordination, and catering for receptions. Status tracked centrally so any staff member can see where each case stands without making calls. Reduces coordination time by 50%.

Online Tribute & Memorial Management

AI assists in publishing online memorial pages, managing condolence message notifications to the family, and generating tribute book content from guestbook entries. Integrated with your website and memorial platform to reduce duplicate data entry.

Michigan Funeral Service Regulatory Context

Michigan Funeral Service Compliance Requirements

Bureau of Professional Licensing (BPL): Michigan funeral establishments are licensed under MCL 339.1801 et seq. and regulated by the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA). AI systems must not perform functions that require a licensed funeral director — all death certificate certifications and arrangement decisions require licensed personnel.

Michigan Preneed Funeral Contracts Act (MCL 328.211 et seq.): Preneed contracts must comply with strict trust fund, disclosure, and cancellation right requirements. AI preneed administration tools must be configured to track Michigan-specific statutory requirements, not generic national preneed frameworks.

Michigan Vital Records (MCL 333.2891 et seq.): Death certificates are filed electronically through Michigan's EVITAL system. AI death certificate preparation tools must produce data formatted for EVITAL submission. The funeral director must certify accuracy before submission — AI produces the draft, not the certification.

FTC Funeral Rule (16 CFR Part 453): The FTC Funeral Rule requires specific price disclosure practices and prohibits certain deceptive practices. AI-generated communications with families must comply with Funeral Rule requirements — particularly around itemized price lists and package disclosure.

Family Communication Sensitivity: All AI-generated communications to bereaved families must be reviewed by funeral home staff before sending. Automated grief support sequences must include an easy opt-out and human escalation path. No AI system should ever send an automated message to a family in the first 72 hours after a death without human review.

5-Step Case Administration Automation for Michigan Funeral Homes

1
Intake to Case Record (Within 30 Minutes of First Call) Family intake information entered once into your funeral management software. AI automatically creates the case record, pre-populates the death certificate template, drafts the obituary, and generates the vendor coordination checklist — all from a single data entry.
2
Death Certificate Preparation (Within 2 Hours) AI drafts the complete Michigan death certificate from case data, flags any missing required fields, cross-references physician contact information for cause of death certification, and generates the EVITAL submission file. Director reviews and certifies. Saves 90 minutes per case.
3
Obituary Draft to Family (Within 4 Hours) AI generates the obituary draft from intake information and family-provided biographical notes. Draft emailed to family contact for review and editing with a simple approval link. Family edits tracked and incorporated automatically. Director reviews final version before publication.
4
Vendor & Permit Coordination (Days 1–3) AI sends coordination messages to all required vendors and agencies based on the service type: casket supplier, crematory, cemetery, florist, permit office. Status updated automatically as confirmations arrive. Any delay immediately flagged to the director.
5
Aftercare Sequence (Day 7 through Month 12) After the service, AI initiates the aftercare sequence: week 1 check-in, month 1 condolence letter, month 6 anniversary acknowledgment, year 1 remembrance. Each piece reviewed and approved by the funeral home before sending. Family opt-out honored immediately.
