Where AI Delivers the Highest ROI in Orthopedics

1. Prior Authorization Automation

AI handles the prior auth workflow end-to-end: generating the clinical summary from chart data, submitting to the payer portal, tracking approval status, sending escalation alerts when auth is delayed past 5 business days, and documenting the authorization number when approved. A process that required a coordinator's full attention now runs in the background. More cases authorized, fewer delays, less staff burnout.

2. Surgical Scheduling Coordination

Orthopedic surgery scheduling is a multi-party coordination problem: the surgeon, the hospital or ASC, anesthesia, the implant rep, and the patient all need to align on a date within a narrow window. AI manages the outreach — sending availability requests to all parties simultaneously, tracking responses, proposing scheduling options, and confirming the date when consensus is reached. What used to require 6 phone calls takes 3 automated messages.

3. Post-Operative Patient Follow-Up

After joint replacement, rotator cuff repair, or ACL reconstruction, patients need structured follow-up at days 3, 10, 21, and 42 post-op. AI sends automated check-ins collecting pain levels, mobility progress, wound healing status, and physical therapy compliance. Responses outside normal parameters trigger immediate escalation to the clinical team. Outcomes improve, readmission rates drop, and patient satisfaction scores rise.

4. Physical Therapy Referral & Compliance Tracking

Orthopedic outcomes are inseparable from PT compliance. AI tracks whether referred patients actually start PT, monitors attendance frequency, and sends reminders to patients who miss sessions. When a patient is 3 weeks post-op and hasn't started PT, AI alerts the care team before the compliance gap becomes a clinical problem.

5. Implant & Equipment Coordination

Orthopedic cases require implant confirmation, sizing verification, and backup option scheduling with vendor reps. AI manages the implant communication workflow: confirming size availability, requesting backup trays, and flagging supply chain issues 7+ days before the scheduled case date — not the morning of.
