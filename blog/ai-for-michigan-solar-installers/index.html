What AI Does for Michigan Solar Installers

Instant Lead Response and Qualification

When a homeowner submits a solar interest form at 10pm on a Tuesday, AI responds within 60 seconds. It asks qualifying questions — home ownership status, monthly electric bill, roof age, shading conditions, interest timeline. By the time your sales rep sees the lead the next morning, it is pre-qualified with a summary, and a site assessment is already scheduled for qualified prospects.

Leads that do not qualify (renters, brand-new roofs that need replacement first, severely shaded sites) receive a polite, helpful response explaining why the timing is not right — preserving your brand while saving your sales team's time.

Automated Proposal Generation

Integrated with satellite imagery and solar modeling tools (Aurora, Enphase Design, or similar), AI generates preliminary proposals — system size, production estimates, 25-year savings projections, incentive calculations, financing options — in minutes instead of hours. Your sales rep arrives at the site assessment with a professional proposal in hand, not returning days later after manually building one in Excel.

Incentive and Financing Calculation

Michigan solar incentives are complex — federal ITC (currently 30%), DTE/Consumers net metering, local utility programs, potential property tax exemptions for solar installations. AI calculates the full incentive stack for each prospect automatically, presents net cost and payback period accurately, and updates calculations when programs change. Prospects who understand the real numbers close at significantly higher rates.

Long-Cycle Follow-Up Nurturing

A 6-8 month sales cycle is hard to maintain manually. Sales reps forget to follow up. Prospects go silent and then surface again when they are ready. AI runs a nurturing sequence across the entire sales cycle — monthly utility bill savings calculations, case studies from Michigan homeowners with similar energy profiles, updates when incentive programs change, and persistent but non-annoying follow-up that keeps your company top of mind when the prospect is finally ready to commit.

Post-Install Review and Referral Generation

Solar customers who are happy with their system are excellent referral sources — they talk about their bill savings to neighbors, post on Nextdoor, and recommend their installer when asked. AI automates the post-install check-in (30 days, 90 days, first annual production report), requests reviews at the moment of maximum satisfaction, and triggers a referral ask with an incentive offer. Solar referral closes at 35% — dramatically higher than cold leads.

Michigan-Specific Opportunities

Commercial and Agricultural Solar

Michigan's agricultural sector — fruit farms, grain operations, large chicken and hog operations — has significant solar opportunity driven by high electricity consumption and available land. Commercial agricultural solar deals are larger ($100K-500K+) and require different qualification criteria and proposal sophistication. AI handles the longer commercial sales cycle with more complex follow-up sequences tailored to agricultural and commercial buyers.

DTE and Consumers Territory Differences

Michigan solar economics differ significantly between DTE Energy and Consumers Energy territories — net metering rates, interconnection timelines, and available programs vary. AI automatically applies the correct utility program calculations based on the prospect address, ensuring proposals are accurate for the customer territory without requiring your sales team to track the differences manually.

The Referral Flywheel

The top-performing solar installers in Michigan generate 35-40% of new business from referrals. Each installed customer who refers one neighbor generates a $25,000 contract at near-zero acquisition cost. AI-driven post-install follow-up and referral requests systematically activate this referral potential — converting satisfied customers into active sales channels. A company installing 8 systems per month that converts 30% of customers to referral sources can generate 2-3 additional installs per month with zero incremental marketing spend.

Going PRO and Green Energy Grants

Michigan Going PRO Talent Fund covers AI training for solar sales and installation teams. Additionally, solar companies with manufacturing or fabrication components may qualify for Industry 4.0 grants. Some Michigan solar installers have also accessed DEQ clean energy business development grants. We evaluate the full grant landscape for every client before building the system.
