Where Michigan Catering Companies Lose Revenue

1. RFPs and Inquiries That Don't Get Acknowledged Fast Enough

A corporate HR manager sends out 3 RFPs for the company holiday party on a Thursday afternoon. The caterer who responds first — with a professional acknowledgment, a question about headcount and dietary restrictions, and a timeline for the full proposal — has a significant advantage. AI sends that response within minutes, day or night.

2. Proposals That Go Cold Without Follow-Up

You spent 3 hours on a wedding proposal. You send it. The couple says they're looking at a few other caterers. That's where most catering companies stop. AI sends a follow-up at Day 3 ("We wanted to check in — do you have any questions about the menu or pricing?"), Day 7 ("We noticed your date is coming up quickly"), and Day 14 ("We have a couple of alternative package options we'd love to show you if budget is a factor"). These follow-ups convert 20–30% of proposals that would otherwise have gone to a competitor.

3. Corporate Clients Who Only Order Once

You cater an incredible corporate lunch. The HR manager loves it. Six months later their company has another event — but they go with whoever they think of first. AI sends a quarterly check-in to every corporate client: "Looking ahead to Q4 — are you planning any events we can support?" Corporate clients with a single point of contact who stays top-of-mind spend 3–5x more than one-time clients.
