Michigan's Veterinary Capacity Crisis

The American Veterinary Medical Association projects a shortage of 15,000 DVMs nationally by 2030, and Michigan is already feeling it. Michigan State University's College of Veterinary Medicine graduates approximately 120 DVMs per year — far fewer than the state's retiring veterinarian cohort. The result: Michigan pet owners are waiting 3–6 weeks for routine appointments at practices that used to offer same-week availability.

The solution isn't hiring a veterinarian you can't find (or afford). It's extracting 2–3 additional patient hours per day from each DVM by eliminating the administrative work that doesn't require a veterinary degree to handle — and AI does this better than adding another front desk staff member.

Six AI Workflows Michigan Veterinary Practices Deploy First

SOAP Note & Medical Record Automation

DVM dictates brief post-exam summary (2–3 min). AI transcribes, structures into SOAP format, populates vitals from exam tech notes, and suggests treatment plan elements. DVM reviews and approves in 90 seconds. Documentation backlog eliminated.

Appointment Scheduling & No-Show Prevention

AI manages online booking, fills cancellation slots from waitlist automatically, sends multi-channel reminders (email + text at 48 hrs and 2 hrs), and handles rescheduling requests 24/7 without staff involvement. No-show rate drops 23%.

Preventive Care Reminder Campaigns

AI tracks every patient's due dates: rabies, DHPP, bordetella, heartworm test, dental cleaning, feline leukemia. Sends personalized outreach at the right time with the right message. Recall compliance jumps — revenue from preventive care increases without adding any new clients.

Discharge Instruction Generation

After each visit, AI generates customized discharge instructions: medications (dose, frequency, duration), activity restrictions, follow-up timeline, and warning signs. Pet owner-friendly language tailored to the specific condition treated. Consistent quality on every discharge, every time.

Prescription Refill Processing

Client requests refill via portal or text. AI reviews patient record against refill eligibility (exam within required window, controlled substance DEA compliance). Routes eligible refills for DVM approval with one click. Flags exceptions for staff follow-up. Saves 30+ minutes of DVM time daily.

Client Communication & Triage

After-hours concern messages triaged by AI: urgency assessment with "go to emergency vet now / schedule tomorrow / monitor at home" guidance, empathetic acknowledgment, and routing to on-call DVM only for actual emergencies. Reduces after-hours DVM interruption by 60%.

SOAP Note Automation: From 15-Minute Burden to 90-Second Approval

The workflow Michigan practices use to recover 2 hours of DVM time per day without sacrificing record quality.

  • 1
    Pre-Exam Record Pull

    As the DVM enters the exam room, AI has already pulled the patient's complete record: last 3 visits, vaccination status, current medications, outstanding recommendations from the last exam, and any client-reported concerns from their intake form. DVM enters the room fully briefed.

  • 2
    Exam Tech Data Capture

    Veterinary tech records vitals, weight, and observations in the practice management system during the exam. AI receives this data in real time — it's already in the record before the DVM dictates. No re-entering information that's already been captured.

  • 3
    Post-Exam DVM Dictation

    After the exam (60–90 seconds while the tech is handling the pet), DVM dictates: findings, assessment, treatment administered, prescription, and follow-up plan. Speaks naturally — the AI understands veterinary clinical language, species-specific terminology, and drug names.

  • 4
    AI Record Completion

    AI generates complete SOAP note: Subjective (client complaint + history), Objective (vitals from tech + physical exam findings from dictation), Assessment (diagnosis or differential), Plan (treatments, prescriptions, recommendations, follow-up). Drug dosages cross-checked against patient weight. Potential interactions flagged.

  • 5
    DVM Review & Simultaneous Discharge Generation

    DVM reviews SOAP note (90 seconds), makes corrections, approves. Simultaneously, AI generates the client discharge instructions in plain language. Both completed before the client reaches the front desk. No end-of-day documentation catch-up. DVM goes home on time.

Michigan Veterinary Client/Patient Data — Privacy Considerations

Veterinary medical records in Michigan are the property of the practice, not the client (unlike human medical records under HIPAA). However, Michigan practices handling client personal data are subject to Michigan Consumer Protection Act and payment card data security requirements. Our AI implementations use appropriate data security standards: encryption at rest and in transit, role-based access controls, and audit logging for all PHI-adjacent data. We build veterinary AI systems with the same security rigor as human healthcare — because your clients trust you with information about the family members they love most.
