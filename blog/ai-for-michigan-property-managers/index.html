What AI Automates for Michigan Property Managers

1. Rental Inquiry Response and Showing Scheduling

When a prospective tenant inquires about a vacant unit — via Zillow, Apartments.com, Facebook Marketplace, or your website — AI responds within 60 seconds with unit details, availability, and self-showing scheduling options. Prospects who don't hear back within an hour are already looking at the next listing. AI ensures no inquiry goes unanswered, even at 11 PM on a Saturday.

2. Tenant Screening Coordination

After a prospective tenant completes an application, AI sends the credit and background check authorization, collects required documents (pay stubs, ID, reference contacts), and compiles a structured applicant summary for your review. Michigan ICRA (Landlord-Tenant law) compliance language is included in all screening communications. You review completed packages — not chasing missing documents.

3. Maintenance Request Triage and Vendor Dispatch

Tenant submits a maintenance request via text, email, or portal. AI categorizes the urgency (emergency vs. routine), acknowledges the request within 60 seconds, assigns to the appropriate vendor from your approved list, confirms the appointment with the tenant, and sends follow-up after completion. Tenants feel heard. Vendors get clear work orders. Managers see a status dashboard — not a voicemail pile.

4. Lease Renewal Campaigns

Michigan tenants are most likely to renew when they're contacted 90–60 days before lease expiration — not 30 days when they've already toured new apartments. AI monitors every lease in your portfolio and triggers a renewal outreach sequence starting at 90 days: personalized renewal offer, any rent adjustment explanation, and a scheduling link for the lease signing. Renewal rates improve 20–35% with proactive AI outreach vs. reactive notice letters.

5. Rent Collection and Late Payment Automation

AI sends rent due reminders 3 days before the 1st, a late notice on the 2nd if unpaid, a follow-up on the 5th, and an escalation flag to the manager on the 7th. Michigan landlord-tenant law requires specific timing and language for late notices — AI enforces this automatically. Late payment rates drop. Collections conversations happen earlier, when solutions still exist.

Michigan Landlord-Tenant Law: Michigan's landlord-tenant law (MCL 554.601-554.640) has specific requirements for security deposit handling, notice periods, and lease termination procedures. AI lease renewal, late notice, and move-out workflows are built with Michigan-compliant language and timing — reducing legal exposure and ensuring consistent compliance across every unit in your portfolio.
