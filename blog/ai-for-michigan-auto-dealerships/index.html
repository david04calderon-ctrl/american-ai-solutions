The Five AI Automation Wins for Michigan Dealerships

After-Hours Lead Response

An AI intake agent responds to every web lead, text, and chat within 60 seconds — 24/7. It qualifies interest, captures vehicle preference, and books a next-day appointment. No lead goes cold overnight.

Service Lane Scheduling & Recall Campaigns

AI cross-references your DMS against NHTSA recall lists and manufacturer service intervals. It automatically reaches customers via text or email with appointment links — no service advisor time required.

F&I Document & Follow-Up Automation

After the desk closes a deal, AI sends docusign sequences, warranty product summaries, and personalized follow-up texts. Unsigned documents get reminder sequences — not forgotten in a stack on the desk.

Used Vehicle Aged Inventory Campaigns

When a used unit crosses 30 days on lot, AI automatically triggers a price-comparison campaign to similar-profile customers in your CRM. At 45 days, it escalates outreach — before you have to take the markdown.

Trade-In Follow-Up Sequences

Customers who get a trade appraisal but don't buy are your warmest unconverted leads. AI sends a structured 5-touch sequence over 14 days — market update, inventory match alert, and a personal check-in from the manager.

CSI Score & Review Capture

24–48 hours after delivery, AI sends a satisfaction survey and Google review request. Unhappy responses are flagged immediately to the GSM — before the OEM survey lands and the score is locked.

F&I: The 72-Hour Window That Dealerships Miss

Every F&I manager knows that a customer who doesn't buy a protection product in the box is rarely going to buy one after they drive away. But what about the 23% of customers who leave without completing all their paperwork — unsigned GAP addendum, unselected warranty tier, missing co-signer ID? Those deals sit in limbo until a desk manager manually hunts them down.

AI F&I Completion Workflow

1
Deal Leaves the Box

2
Hour 4 — Friendly Reminder

Customer receives a text: "Congrats on your new [vehicle]! We have a couple of quick document items to wrap up. Here's your secure link to complete them from your phone — takes 3 minutes."

3

5

AI sends a satisfaction check-in + CSI survey + Google review request. Negative feedback is routed to the GSM. Positive sentiment triggers the Google review link.

Used Vehicle Inventory: AI vs. The Clock

Every day a used vehicle sits on your lot costs you floor plan interest, detail maintenance, and the creeping pressure to markdown. AI doesn't replace your used vehicle manager's instincts — it executes the follow-through that there's never enough time to do manually.

Day 1–15: Match & Alert

AI compares new inventory against your existing customer base — who traded a similar vehicle, who browsed similar inventory online, who's in a lease ending within 90 days.

Day 16–30: Active Campaign

Matched customers receive a personalized text: "We just took in a [Year/Make/Model] with [mileage] that matches what you've been looking at. Want to be first to see it?"

Day 31–45: Urgency Trigger

At 31 days, AI escalates: price comparison against market, "vehicles like this are averaging 22 days on market right now" message, and an invitation to make an offer before public pricing.

Michigan-Specific Context: Why This Market Is Different

Michigan dealerships are not operating in a generic market. The OEM relationships here create unique pressures that AI can directly address:

  • Model year transitions. Michigan Tier 1 supplier relationships mean dealerships often have advance notice of production changes — AI can help convert existing owners to new models before public launch.
  • Conquest from plant closings. When a supplier group downsizes or shifts, affected employees are often in the market. AI can identify geographic clusters and target them.
  • Fleet and commercial accounts. Michigan's industrial base means fleet inquiries come in at odd hours. AI captures these while BDC staff are focused on retail floor traffic.
  • Seasonal service spikes. Michigan winters hit hard — AI can run pre-winter tire, battery, and brake campaigns timed to the first frost forecast in each region of the state.

What AI Does Not Do at a Dealership

Before any Michigan dealer principals call us, let's be clear about what AI does not replace:

  • It does not replace your F&I manager — it handles document follow-up, not the product presentation
  • It does not negotiate deals — it books appointments and captures intent, then hands off to sales staff
  • It does not replace your BDC team — it handles overflow and after-hours coverage, and amplifies what your team can do
  • It does not make credit decisions — it works downstream of RouteOne/DealerTrack, not inside them
  • It does not replace the test drive — it just makes sure the customer shows up for one

The goal is simple: make sure that every lead your advertising generates actually gets contacted, every appointment your service department schedules actually gets confirmed, and every deal your sales team closes actually gets completed documentation. The humans do the relationship work. AI makes sure nothing falls through the cracks.
