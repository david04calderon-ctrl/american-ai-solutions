Michigan Weather Creates Roofing Demand — Speed Captures It

Michigan averages 8–12 significant hail and wind events per year across the Lower Peninsula. After each event, thousands of homeowners file insurance claims and simultaneously contact 3–5 roofing companies for estimates. The market clears within 72 hours — the roofers who respond first and follow up best win the jobs. The ones who call back two days later get the voicemail.

AI makes your company the one that responds first, follows up consistently, and closes more of the jobs you already earned.

A Michigan roofing company booking just 3 additional replacement jobs per month from improved lead follow-up adds $532,800 in annual revenue at a $14,800 average ticket — on jobs that were already in the pipeline and already lost to silence.

Instant Lead Response (60-Second Rule)

When a homeowner submits a form on your website, Google LSA, HomeAdvisor, or Angi, AI sends an immediate text within 60 seconds: "Hi [name], thanks for reaching out to [Company]. We're scheduling free storm inspections in your area this week — does [day] or [day] work for you?" Homeowners who get this response book at 3x the rate of those who wait for a callback during business hours.

For storm canvassing leads, AI sends a follow-up text to every door-knocked address that gave a number: "We knocked on your door today — we're offering free storm damage inspections in [neighborhood]. Want us to get on your roof this week?"

Estimate Follow-Up Sequences

After an estimate is sent, most Michigan roofers do one follow-up call and give up. AI runs a structured sequence:

  • 24 hours: "Your estimate is in your inbox — do you have any questions about the scope or materials?"
  • 3 days: "We're scheduling installs for [month] — want to lock in your slot before the season fills up?"
  • 7 days: Insurance-specific message if applicable — "Have you heard back from your adjuster? We work directly with all Michigan carriers and can handle the supplement process."
  • 14 days: Final follow-up with a limited-time scheduling incentive

Insurance Supplement Tracking and Communication

Insurance roofing jobs in Michigan often require supplements — additional line items added after the initial adjuster scope (code upgrades, ice and water shield, drip edge per Michigan Residential Code R905.2.8.2). AI tracks every open insurance claim, monitors supplement status, and sends updates to homeowners: "We've submitted your supplement to [carrier] — we'll notify you as soon as they respond." Homeowners who feel informed during the insurance process refer more and review better.

Job Completion and Review Generation

48 hours after job completion, AI sends a review request: "Your new roof is installed — we hope everything looks great. If you have a moment, a Google review means the world to a local Michigan company like ours." Roofing companies that automate review requests see Google review volume triple within one season — directly driving more organic leads from "roofing contractor near me" searches.

Referral and Neighbor Campaigns

After a roof replacement in a subdivision, AI sends canvassing messages to neighboring addresses (from your existing contact list or door-knock data): "We just finished a roof on [Street] — we're offering free inspections to neighbors while we're in the area this week." This turns every completed job into a neighborhood prospecting opportunity.

Off-Season Demand Generation

Michigan roofing slows in December–February but doesn't stop. AI sends winter campaigns to past estimate leads who didn't close: "February is the best time to book your spring roof — no weather delays, lower scheduling pressure, and we can lock in this year's material pricing." Spring scheduling campaigns go out in January to past customers due for maintenance: "Michigan winters are hard on flashing and gutters — want us to do a quick spring inspection?"

Michigan Roofing Compliance Notes

  • Michigan Residential Code R905.2.8.2: Ice barrier (ice and water shield) required in Michigan for the first 24 inches from eave — a common supplement line item worth $800–$1,500 per job
  • Michigan contractor licensing (MCL 339.2401): Residential roofing requires a Residential Builder or Maintenance and Alteration Contractor license from LARA — AI can track license expiration and send renewal alerts to your license holder
  • Public Adjuster rules (MCL 500.1261 et seq.): Roofing contractors cannot act as public adjusters — AI communication templates are reviewed to ensure they don't cross this line
  • TCPA: All SMS lead follow-up requires prior express consent — typically captured in the lead form or door-knock sign-up
