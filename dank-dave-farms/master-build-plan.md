## Phase 1: Location Decision

### Option A — Basement Build (Start Here)
**Timeline:** 6–10 weeks to first seeds
**Cost:** $8,000–$18,000 all-in

Pros:
- No building permit in most Michigan jurisdictions for basement conversion
- Naturally cooler temps = easier to manage heat from LEDs
- Privacy — no neighbors, no exterior visibility
- Starts revenue/content cycle immediately

Setup requirements:
- Dedicate 1–2 rooms (minimum 10×10 per room, ideal 10×20 total)
- Add 200A subpanel circuit if running 2+ HLG Diablo fixtures
- Install AC duct extension or mini-split (Mitsubishi MSZ-GL single zone preferred)
- Seal and paint walls white (Bright White semi-gloss — maximizes light reflection)
- Run 4" or 6" carbon filter exhaust through rim joist / window well

### Option B — Pole Barn Build (Next Property / Expansion)
**Timeline:** 3–6 months to first seeds
**Cost:** $35,000–$80,000 all-in depending on size and insulation

Best for:
- 4+ rooms running simultaneously
- Michigan Craft Grower license (Tier 1: up to 500 plants)
- Full commercial operation with dedicated veg/flower/drying/trim/storage rooms
- Easier camera mounting, mechanical room, water/drain infrastructure

Build spec:
- 30×40 or 40×60 insulated pole barn (R-19 walls, R-38 ceiling minimum)
- Dedicated mechanical room (dehumidifiers, HVAC, water treatment, nutrient reservoirs)
- Separate veg room (300–400 sq ft), flower room 1 (600+ sq ft), flower room 2 (600+ sq ft)
- Drying room with precise humidity control (55–62% RH, 60–65°F)
- Camera/server closet for automation hub

**Recommendation:** Start with basement (Phase 1), document the build and grows on content, fund the pole barn build from sponsorship + sales revenue by Year 2.

## Mr. Canucks Living Soil Recipe (Gaia Green Method)

This is the core grow philosophy. No synthetic nutrients, no pH pH drama, no daily feeding — the soil feeds the plant.

### Base Mix
- 70% high-quality aerated compost base (Coast of Maine Stonington Blend or equivalent)
- 20% perlite (drainage/aeration)
- 10% worm castings (microbial inoculant, base nutrition)

### Gaia Green Dry Amendments (per cubic foot of soil)
- **4-4-4 All Purpose** — 1 tbsp/gallon for base charge
- **2-8-4 Power Bloom** — added at flip (week 1 of flower): 1 tbsp/gallon top dress
- Optional top dress at week 4 flower: light 4-4-4 if plants show hunger

### Watering Protocol (Mr. Canucks Method)
- Water only (no liquid nutrients in living soil)
- Water to 20% runoff on dry cycle (lift pot method — water when pot feels 30–40% of its saturated weight)
- Never oversaturate — let soil dry between waterings
- pH water to 6.5–6.8 for soil (living soil is more forgiving but still benefits from pH management)

### Amendment Top Dresses (Optional Boosters)
- **Kelp meal** — trace minerals, root health
- **Crustacean meal** — chitin (IPM + phosphorus)
- **Insect frass** — nitrogen, microbial activity
- **Mycorrhizal inoculant** at transplant (Mykos or equivalent)

### Flush Protocol
- No aggressive flushing in living soil — the organic compounds are what you want
- Last 10–14 days: water only (no additional amendments)
- Trichome check: 20–30% amber for relaxing effect, 10–15% amber for energetic effect

## Genetics Strategy

Seed sources and approach (following Mr. Canucks philosophy of hunting phenos):
- **Seed banks:** Ethos Genetics, Exotic Genetix, Compound Genetics, Oni Seed Co, Cannarado
- **Hunt phenos:** Pop 5–10 seeds of each strain, select 2–3 keepers for mother plants
- **Cloning:** Take cuts from mothers at week 3 veg — maintain mother room in veg room
- **Strain rotation:** Run 2 proven cultivars per flower cycle + 1 new genetic hunt
- **Genetic tracking:** Full data card per plant (see genetics-tracking-system.md)

## Michigan Legal Notes

**Personal cultivation (no license needed):**
- Adults 21+ may grow up to 12 cannabis plants at a residential property
- Plants must not be visible from public space
- Marijuana must be kept in enclosed, locked space (exterior structures)
- Cannot sell without a state license

**If going commercial (Craft Grower License):**
- Apply through Michigan CRA (cra.michigan.gov)
- Tier 1: 100 plants, Tier 2: 500 plants, Tier 3: 1,500 plants
- Class C Grower license: unlimited canopy (medical/adult-use)
- City/township zoning approval required before state license
- This is the path to Dank Dave Farms as a real brand and business entity
